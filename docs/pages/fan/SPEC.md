# fan.html — Page Specification
**Last updated: 2026-03-16**
**Authority: V8 strategy process — Stage 1: Purpose**

---

## Stage 1: Purpose — The One Job

**The fan dashboard's one sentence:**

> Show fans what is happening with the artists they chose to follow, so they never miss something they would have cared about — and make them feel close, not informed.

That word "close" is load-bearing. Information platforms tell you what happened. fan.html makes you feel like you're still part of it.

---

## What this page is

fan.html is the fan's personal space on ABLE. It is the answer to the question every fan asks after signing up on an artist's page: *"What happens now?"*

The answer must be: *"You're in. Here's where we are."*

It is not a social network. It is not a content feed. It is not Instagram or TikTok. It is not Spotify's algorithmic suggestion engine. It is not Patreon's content subscription model.

It is a place where fans go because they care about specific artists they chose to follow. Every item they see is from someone they actively wanted to hear from. Nothing is ranked. Nothing is promoted. Nothing is generated by a machine.

The closest analogy is not a product analogy. It's a human one: it's like having a small, well-kept notebook where you write down the artists you love and everything you want to remember about what they're up to. fan.html is that notebook — except it updates itself when your artists do something real.

---

## Primary job

**Show fans what is happening with the artists they chose to follow, so they never miss something they would have cared about.**

That is the entire job. One sentence. Do not expand it. Do not layer discovery on top of it until that primary job is done perfectly.

Secondary jobs, in order:
1. Help fans find more artists they might genuinely like — via taste (genre / city / sound), not engagement metrics
2. Show fans what is playing near them, so a show they want to attend is never missed because they didn't know about it
3. Provide a space for Close Circle — the fans who go a little further

---

## The three things fan.html must make fans feel

1. **Close** — not distant, not mediated, not algorithmic. The artist's world comes to them directly. Nothing gets filtered out.
2. **First to know** — not catching up after seeing it on social media. Here before everyone else.
3. **That their relationship with the artist is real** — not transactional, not managed. A genuine connection between two people across a screen.

---

## The three things fan.html must not feel like

1. **Another social media feed** — infinite, optimised, noisy
2. **A news aggregator** — generic, impersonal, same-energy as reading a website
3. **A notification centre** — administrative, functional, cold

---

## The conduit principle applied to the fan dashboard

The root truth of ABLE: "The relationship between artist and fan belongs to them. Not to a platform."

On fan.html this means:

- The fan feels like they are looking at the artist's world, not ABLE's product
- Each artist appears in their own accent colour — the fan moves between artist-owned spaces, not a generic platform feed
- The vocabulary is the artist's: "New from Nova Reign" not "Nova Reign posted"
- ABLE is the door. The artists are the room.

This is the conduit principle in action. ABLE holds the door open and then steps out of the way.

---

## What fan.html must feel like

- A clean, personal morning briefing from the artists you actually care about
- Warm. Specific. Direct. Not noisy.
- The same design world as the artist profiles they signed up from — dark, considered, quiet
- Something an artist would be proud to have their fans land on
- Worth opening once a week, reliably

---

## What fan.html must not do

- Surface content based on engagement metrics or popularity scores
- Show any kind of global trending or viral content
- Use the word "feed", "post", "content", "followers", "engagement"
- Manufacture scarcity or urgency
- Make the fan feel like they are being managed or targeted
- Gamify fan behaviour (badges, streaks, points, leaderboards)
- Show the fan to other fans
- Allow direct messages fan-to-fan or fan-to-artist (not a community platform)
- Show advertising of any kind
- Require the fan to check it daily to get value from it

---

## Sections, in order

1. **Header** — ABLE wordmark + notification bell + profile icon. Conditional sub-greeting when there is something genuinely new.
2. **Content tabs** — Following / Discover / Near me. Simple, honest. No "For You" tab. No "Home."
3. **Following view** (default):
   - Pre-release countdown strips (when followed artists have future releases)
   - Today strip — items from followed artists in the last 24 hours
   - This week strip — items from followed artists in the last 7 days, beyond today
   - Close Circle section — per supported artist (Phase 2 with Stripe)
   - Cold-start suggestions row — on first visit with 1 followed artist
   - Caught-up state — when all items have been seen
   - Empty state — when the fan follows nobody
4. **Discover view**:
   - Filter pills: Connected (default) / New to ABLE / By sound / Just dropped
   - Artist cards with honest "why" reasons (credit connection, genre match, location)
   - "The people behind the music" section — producers, mixers, collaborators credited on artists the fan follows
5. **Near me view**:
   - Location display with edit option (city-level, manually set, no GPS request)
   - Shows grouped by Tonight / This week / Coming up
   - Both followed artists and discovery artists with shows nearby
   - Empty state if no shows in city
6. **Bottom tab bar** — Following / Artists / Me. Three tabs only.

## Technical constraints

- Single HTML file. No build pipeline. No npm.
- localStorage first. Supabase when backend lands (Phase 2)
- DM Sans only. Same font as able-v7.html artist profiles.
- Dark theme only for v1. `--color-bg: #0d0e1a`
- Platform accent: `#8b7cf4` (distinct from artist accent — this is ABLE's neutral, not an artist colour)
- Each artist displayed in their own accent colour, applied via inline style from data
- Mobile-first: 390px shell width, min 44px tap targets, no horizontal scroll at 375px
- Performance: LCP ≤ 2.5s, CLS ≤ 0.10 — render from localStorage immediately, no API wait

---

## Auth state (v1 — current)

v1 fan.html is localStorage-only. Demo data renders when no real data exists. This is intentional — the page must work and feel good before real data is connected.

Real auth (Phase 2): Supabase magic link → fan identity bound → real followed-artists data from `fan_follows` table → real moments from `moments` table → real shows from `shows` table.

## Tone and voice rules

Fan-facing copy is different from artist-facing copy but lives in the same register.

- Direct, warm, specific
- Never "your feed" — say "the artists you're following"
- Never "posted" — say "dropped", "just released", "playing tonight"
- Never "content" — say "music", "show", "update from the artist"
- Dates and times are specific. "Tonight, 8pm" not "upcoming event"
- Empty states are honest, not cheerful. "Nothing new today." not "All quiet on the western front!"
- Discovery is honest about why. "Similar to artists you follow" not "Recommended for you"

---

## Relationship to other pages

- Fan arrives here after signing up on able-v7.html (the artist profile) — via `?artist=slug&ref=signup` URL param
- fan.html links back to able-v7.html when a fan taps an artist or feed item — with `?src=fan-dashboard` tag
- fan.html does not link to admin.html — completely separate user type
- Close Circle on fan.html connects to payment flow (Phase 2 — Stripe)
- The ABLE wordmark uses a view transition to slide between artist profiles and the fan dashboard (P2 — Chrome 126+)

---

## What success looks like

A fan opens fan.html on a Tuesday morning and in 10 seconds knows:
- Did any artist they follow release something new this week?
- Is anyone they follow playing near them soon?
- Is there anyone new they might like?

They get the answer to all three questions without scrolling more than once. They tap through to the artist's profile when something catches them. They close the app feeling like they stayed close without being overwhelmed.

That is the product. Everything else is detail.

---

## The empty state moment

This is the most important moment on the entire page. Not the richest-data view. Not the Close Circle invitation. The empty state.

A fan who just signed up from an artist's page has one followed artist and nothing is new today. They land on fan.html expecting to find... something. What they find is one line:

> You're in. [Artist name] doesn't post often — but when they do, you'll be the first to know. This is where it lands.

That is the emotional register. "I'm glad I signed up. Something good is coming." The empty state must create this feeling every time. It is the primary copy challenge on the page. It is where most fan dashboards fail.

See COPY.md for all empty state variations with full context-sensitivity.
