# ABLE — Core Product Truth
**Status: ACTIVE — Layer 0. Nothing overrides this.**
**Last updated: 2026-03-14**

---

## What ABLE is

A conversion profile for independent artists.

The artist puts their ABLE link in their bio. A fan taps it. In the next 30 seconds, that fan streams the latest track, sees the next show, signs up to hear about the release — all without leaving the page, all without an algorithm deciding what they see.

**That's it. That's the whole product.**

---

## The internal product truth

**The relationship between artist and fan belongs to them. Not to a platform.**

Every social platform — Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Spotify — has the same business model: they own the audience relationship. They decide who sees what. They can change the algorithm tomorrow. The artist has no list, no direct line, no ownership.

ABLE inverts this. When a fan signs up on ABLE, that email address belongs to the artist. When ABLE shuts down (as all platforms eventually do), the artist exports their list and leaves. The relationship survives the platform.

This is not a marketing angle. It is the reason the product exists.

---

## What the website (landing.html) must prove

Three things, in this order:

1. **Your page is beautiful.** Not "professional." Not "clean." Beautiful. The phone mockup does this job. It must show a real, specific artist's world — not a generic template.

2. **Your fans can actually do something here.** Stream the music. Get a ticket. Sign up. The page does things. It is not a list of links.

3. **The relationship stays yours.** The fan list belongs to the artist. Zero ABLE cut on support income. Export any time. This is the thing that separates ABLE from every alternative.

## Three user journeys (do not conflate)

### 1. Artist
`start.html` → `able-v6.html` (their live page) → `admin.html` (dashboard)

The artist is the product's primary customer. Everything is designed around giving them control, confidence, and real data — without making them a marketer.

### 2. Fan
`able-v6.html` (from artist's social bio link)

The fan doesn't know or care what ABLE is. They clicked a link. They should feel like they landed somewhere the artist actually built — not a generic service page.

### 3. Freelancer (producer / mixer / videographer)
`freelancer-start.html` → `freelancer.html` → admin variant

Discovered via credits on artist release cards. Different use case entirely: credits, rate card, portfolio, booking CTA. No campaign states, no fan capture, no top card.

## What ABLE is not

- **Not a social network.** No feed. No followers. No engagement metrics. No algorithm.
- **Not a marketing tool.** The artist is not "growing their audience." They are staying close to people who showed up.
- **Not a template builder.** There are no templates. The profile is shaped by genre, feel, and current moment — not selected from a gallery.
- **Not a link aggregator.** If content can be experienced inside the page, it must be. Linking out is the fallback, not the default. (See `core/V6_BUILD_AUTHORITY.md §17` — the Conduit Principle.)
- **Not a CRM dashboard.** The fan list is described as "your list, your relationship." It is not called a CRM. It is not gamified. It is people who want to hear from you.

## Tier model (definition — not gates)

| Tier | Price | Who it's for |
|---|---|---|
| Free | £0 | Artist testing the water. Looks genuinely personal. Not a demo. |
| Artist | £9/mo | Artist taking it seriously. Full fan list, campaign modes. |
| Artist Pro | £19/mo | Artist with a real audience. CRM, broadcasts, advanced tools. |
| Label | £49/mo | Small label, management. Up to 10 artist pages. |

Premium profiles look **precisely dialled**, not just "more features." Free profiles look **genuinely personal**, not like a downgrade.

ABLE positioning: replaces Linktree Pro (£8/mo) + a basic email tool (£10/mo). Net saving, more capability, owns the relationship.

## What the product must never be

- An algorithm that decides what fans see
- A platform that owns the fan relationship
- A service with 20% revenue cuts
- A tool that makes artists feel like marketers
- A page that looks like every other link-in-bio

---

## The three things fans feel (not features — feelings)

1. **"I'm close to this artist."** Not "I'm on a platform." The page is the artist's world, not a neutral service page.
2. **"I can actually do something here."** Stream. Buy a ticket. Sign up. Real actions, not a list of links.
3. **"This wasn't made by a template."** Every profile has a personality. The identity system makes this structurally true.
