## LINKTREE — The incumbent to beat

**Scale:** 181.4M monthly visits. ~1.3 billion clicks/month. Dominant brand recognition.

**Their pitch:** "One link. Everything you do online."

**What users actually complain about (from Capterra reviews):**
- **Account suspensions without warning** — artists lose their entire page and fan list overnight, no explanation, no appeal
- **Poor customer support** — non-responsive, generic replies, no human contact
- **Billing issues** — unexpected charges, renewal notifications not sent
- **Analytics are terrible** — click counts only, marketed as premium feature but actually basic
- **No ownership** — if Linktree shuts you down, you have nothing

**The attack surface for ABLE:**
Linktree's #1 complaint is that artists are building on land they don't own. Account suspension without warning is the nightmare scenario ABLE exists to solve. Every artist who has been burned by this is a perfect ABLE acquisition. "Your page doesn't get suspended. Your fan list can't be taken from you. Export everything, any time."

---

## BEACONS — The fast-growing challenger

**Scale:** 21.4M visits/month, growing ~10% monthly. Significant threat.

**Their pitch:** Full creator toolkit — links, store, email, analytics, AI.

**What users actually complain about:**
- **9% transaction fees on free tier** — 34% of negative reviews cite this. Every sale, every tip, Beacons takes 9%.
- **Email tools suspended without reason** — accounts flagged, no appeal process, emails stop sending
- **Support takes 4–7 business days** — completely unusable for a time-sensitive platform
- **No music-specific features** — they serve YouTubers, TikTokers, podcasters. Musicians are an afterthought.
- **No team access, no multi-artist management**

**The attack surface for ABLE:**
Beacons' 9% fee is ABLE's 0% fee's direct target. An artist selling £5 support packs: Beacons takes 45p per sale. ABLE takes 0. That's a direct, quantifiable claim. Also: Beacons is generic creator tool — no campaign states, no gig mode, no pre-release countdown. A musician is just another creator to them.

---

## FEATURE.FM — The music specialist, expensive

**Scale:** Traffic not publicly reported (suggests limited adoption).

**Their pitch:** Smart links and pre-save campaigns for music.

**What they do well:** Pre-save campaigns, regional platform tracking, Spotify/Apple/SoundCloud integration. Purpose-built for music.

**Weaknesses:**
- Very expensive — up to $199/mo. Indie artists can't afford this.
- Traffic data suggests low adoption outside of mid-tier/major label artists
- No fan email capture — it's link routing, not relationship building
- No campaign states, no page identity, no vibe system

**The attack surface for ABLE:**
Feature.fm owns the "smart link" category but not the "artist profile + fan relationship" category. They're a tool for routing streams, not building an audience. ABLE is cheaper (£9/mo vs Feature.fm's equivalent) and adds everything Feature.fm doesn't have.

## ABLE'S COMPETITIVE POSITION

### The three direct attack lines:

**1. Against Linktree:** "Your Linktree can be suspended tomorrow. Your ABLE page is yours — your fans, your data, your emails. Export everything, any time. We don't own your audience."

**2. Against Beacons:** "Beacons takes 9% of every sale you make. ABLE takes 0%. If you sell £500 in support packs this year, Beacons takes £45. ABLE takes nothing."

**3. Against Feature.fm:** "Feature.fm routes clicks. ABLE builds relationships. Your fan's email address in your list is worth more than 1,000 clicks to Spotify."

### ABLE's unique advantages no competitor has:
1. **Campaign state machine** — page shifts automatically with release/gig. None of them have this.
2. **0% on everything** — email, fan data, support. Beacons 9%, others take cuts. ABLE 0%.
3. **Data portability as a core promise** — not a buried feature, the headline value prop
4. **Music identity** — 7 vibes, genre-specific design, feels like music. Not "creator tools."
5. **Fan relationship system** — email list that belongs to the artist, not the platform

### The real opportunity:
Koji is dead. The "interactive mini-app" creator left a gap. Linktree has support and suspension problems driving artists to look for alternatives. Beacons has fee resentment. Feature.fm is too expensive. The market is actively searching for a music-first alternative — ABLE needs to be the answer they find.
