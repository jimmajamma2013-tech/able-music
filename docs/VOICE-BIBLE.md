# ABLE — Voice Bible
**Created: 2026-03-16 | For: developers, contractors, writers, anyone who ships copy.**

Pick this up and you can write in ABLE's voice within 5 minutes. For the full reference, read `docs/systems/copy/SPEC.md`.

---

## The 3 Principles

**1. Say one thing, then stop.**
Every piece of copy does one job. When it has done that job, it ends. No padding. No reassurance. No "and also." The full stop is intentional.

**2. The artist is the subject. ABLE is the door.**
On the artist's page, ABLE is invisible. The copy sounds like the artist wrote it. On the admin, ABLE speaks like a smart manager — warm, direct, never cheerleading. The platform never inserts itself between the artist and their fans.

**3. Specific is always better than general.**
"12 people tapped your Spotify link today" is better than "Your clicks are up." "You have 23 fans in Manchester" is better than "Advanced analytics available." Specificity is respect. Vagueness is noise.

---

## The Register in 10 Words

Honest. Direct. Warm. Understated. Specific. Dry. Present. Artist-first. Unhurried. Real.

---

## 5 Rules with Examples

### Rule 1: Write in the artist's voice on the profile page.

The fan should believe the artist typed every word. ABLE is invisible.

| Don't write this | Write this instead |
|---|---|
| "This artist's fan sign-up is powered by ABLE." | "Stay close." |
| "Subscribe to receive updates from this artist." | "I'll only reach out when something's actually happening." |

---

### Rule 2: No exclamation marks in any ABLE-written copy. Ever.

Exclamation marks make the platform sound like a cheerleader. Artists do not want a cheerleader. The only exception: the artist has written it themselves in their own bio or snap card content.

| Don't write this | Write this instead |
|---|---|
| "Your page is live! Share it with your fans!" | "Your page is live." |
| "3 new fans this week! Great work!" | "3 fans this week — that's new." |

---

### Rule 3: Empty states describe what will happen, not what hasn't.

An empty state is a reason to act, not a confession of emptiness.

| Don't write this | Write this instead |
|---|---|
| "No shows added yet. Click + to add a show." | "Playing anywhere soon? Your fans want to know →" |
| "No fans yet. Start building your audience." | "When fans sign up on your page, they'll appear here. Your list. Your relationship." |

---

### Rule 4: Tier gates describe what the artist gains, not what they're missing.

Never gate with "Upgrade to unlock." Always say the specific thing they get, then the price.

| Don't write this | Write this instead |
|---|---|
| "Upgrade to unlock email broadcasts." | "Write to your fans directly. Not a broadcast. Not a newsletter. Just you and the people who signed up. From £19/month." |
| "Pro feature. Upgrade to continue." | "You have 23 fans in Manchester. Upgrade to see who they are before your show there." |

---

### Rule 5: Toasts and confirmations are one word or two. Past tense. Period.

After an action completes, confirm it with minimum words. Redundancy is a tell that the platform is nervous.

| Don't write this | Write this instead |
|---|---|
| "Your profile has been saved successfully!" | "Saved." |
| "Link copied to clipboard." | "Copied." |
| "Show has been added to your events list." | "Show added." |

## The Single Test

**"Would an independent artist with real credibility be embarrassed if this appeared on their page?"**

If yes — rewrite. If no — ship.

Run this test on every piece of copy before it goes into code. Applies to: button labels, empty states, toast copy, onboarding questions, error messages, tier gate copy, and section headers.

## Banned Phrases (the 20 most important)

These phrases are banned from all user-facing copy. No exceptions.

1. "Turn fans into superfans"
2. "Grow your audience"
3. "Monetise your fanbase" / "Monetize"
4. "Engage your followers" / "Engage with your fans"
5. "Content creator" — say "artist"
6. "Going viral" / "Viral"
7. "Unlock" preceding anything
8. "Get started" as a CTA
9. "You're all set!" / "Welcome aboard!"
10. "Superfan" — internal vocabulary only, never user-facing
11. "Dashboard" in any visible UI label or page title
12. "Mailing list" / "Subscribers" / "Newsletter"
13. "Boost" / "Supercharge" / "Level up" / "Skyrocket"
14. "Leverage" / "Power up"
15. "Content" when referring to music or art — say "music", "update", "message"
16. "Followers" — say "fans" or "people who follow them"
17. "Community" when meaning audience — say "your fans"
18. "Exclusive content" / "Premium access" / "Exclusive benefits"
19. "Become a supporter" — say "Come closer"
20. "Sign up" as a CTA on the fan-facing page — say "I'm in" / "Stay close" / "Count me in"

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Fan sign-up heading | Warm, first-person artist voice | "Stay close." |
| Fan sign-up confirmation | Artist-voiced, brief, no drama | "You're in. I'll keep you close." |
| Admin greeting | One beat. Warm but done. | "Good to see you, James." |
| Admin sub-greeting | Specific, observational | "3 days until Somewhere Else drops." |
| Error message | Calm, specific, gives a path forward | "Check your email address." |
| Empty state (admin) | Honest, connects to purpose | "Playing anywhere soon? Your fans want to know →" |
| Empty state (fan-facing) | Silent — hide the section entirely | *(no text rendered)* |
| Upgrade prompt | Says what the artist gains + the price | "Write to your fans directly. From £19/month." |
| Toast / confirmation | One or two words. Past tense. Period. | "Saved." / "Show added." / "Copied." |
| Onboarding question | A question, not a label | "What do you go by?" not "Artist Name:" |
| Done screen | The minimum true statement | "Your page is live." |
| Destructive action | Calm, specific, says what is lost | "This removes your page and all fan data. This cannot be undone." |
