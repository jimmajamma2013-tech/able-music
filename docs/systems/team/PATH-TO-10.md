## What gets it to 10/10

### Gap 1: Equity and financial incentives not fully specified (−0.3)

The document mentions equity "to be considered at Series A" but gives no framework. The first two or three hires will ask about this. Without a clear position, the answer sounds evasive.

**Fix:**
Add a sub-section under Section 7 (Hiring principles → Offer) with a clear position:
> "At this stage, ABLE does not offer equity to early employees. If and when a funding round happens, early employees will be considered for a retrospective options grant. This is a commitment, not a guarantee."

This is more honest and more trustworthy than a vague "to be considered".

### Gap 2: No exit/offboarding section (−0.2)

The document handles growth well but doesn't address what happens when a hire doesn't work out. The 3-month probation is mentioned but the process around it is absent.

**Fix:**
Add a short section:
> "If it's not working by Month 2 of probation, say so clearly — don't wait until Month 3. Feedback must be specific and documented. Separation, when it happens, is handled cleanly: final pay on time, no drama, reference provided if earned."

### Gap 3: No onboarding checklist for first two hires (−0.2)

There's an instruction to listen to artist calls before Day 1, but no structured first-week plan.

**Fix:**
Add a lightweight first-week checklist for each role (Day 1: accounts set up, Day 1: read these 3 docs, Week 1: shadow 2 artist calls, etc.)

### Gap 4: Contractor / freelancer path not addressed (−0.1)

Between "James alone" and "first hire" there is likely a gap period where a freelancer handles specific tasks (e.g., a copywriter for the newsletter, a designer for a one-off asset). This is not covered.

**Fix:**
Add a paragraph in Section 1 about the freelancer phase: when to use a freelancer, what to use them for, and what not to use them for (never outsource artist calls to a freelancer — the relationship must be human and owned).
