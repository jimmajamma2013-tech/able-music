## The final confirmation (read this on the day you decide to resign)

If you are reading this because the trigger is reached and the resignation letter is in drafts — stop. Read these questions slowly.

**1. Is this the right time, not just the right number?**
The MRR trigger is the financial condition. But the right time also means: C5/C6 is stable, the key relationships are not in acute crisis, and you are in a high state, not a low one. The number can be reached at the wrong moment. If the number is right but something else is acutely wrong, it is worth waiting 4 weeks.

**2. Do you have 6 months of personal expenses in liquid cash?**
Not investments. Not ISA. Cash in an instant-access savings account. If this is not full, the exit is premature.

**3. Can ABLE survive 2 weeks without you?**
Automations running. No P0 bugs. If you were unreachable for 14 days, the revenue would continue. If the answer is no — fix the fragility first.

**4. Do you have a specific conviction about the next 90 days?**
"In the next 90 days I am going to do [one thing] and I believe it will [measurable outcome]." If you cannot say this, spend one more week doing the thinking before exiting.

**5. Do at least 2 people outside the business know what you are doing and believe in it?**
Not because external validation is required. Because Year 1 is hard and you will need people who remember why you did this when you temporarily forget.

If all 5 answers are yes: the letter is ready. It has been in drafts for at least 4 weeks. Read it once. Make any small edits. Send it.

Then call the 5 people.

Then post.

Then email your founding artists.

Then open something good and let the day be what it is.
