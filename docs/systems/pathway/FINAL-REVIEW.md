# Final Review — The Last Thing to Read Before You Hand In Notice
**Created: 2026-03-16**
**Read: Once. On the day you decide to resign.**

---

## You have reached the trigger

If you are reading this document, the pre-shift checklist is complete, the MRR has been at £5k+ for 3 consecutive months, and the decision is in front of you.

This is not a checklist. The checklists are done. This is a moment to stop, think clearly, and confirm.

---

## The confirmation questions

Read each of these slowly. Answer honestly. If any answer is "no" or "I'm not sure," pause and address it before sending the resignation letter.

**1. Is this the right time, not just the right number?**

The MRR trigger is the financial condition. But the right time is also a health condition, a personal condition, and an emotional condition. Are you in a relatively stable period — not perfect, but not acute? Is C5/C6 manageable right now? Are the key relationships in your life stable?

The number can be hit at the wrong time. If the number is right but something else is in acute crisis, it is worth waiting 4 weeks.

**2. Do you have 6 months of personal expenses in liquid cash?**

Not investments. Not ISA. Cash in an instant-access savings account.

If this is not full, the exit is premature. The emergency fund is not optional. It is the thing that determines whether you make good decisions during Year 1 or panicked ones.

**3. Is there a clear next step in the product — not just a to-do list, but a specific conviction?**

You should be able to say: "In the next 90 days, I am going to do [one specific thing] and I believe it will [measurable outcome]."

If you cannot say this, spend one more week in the job doing the thinking. Do not exit into indefinite ambiguity.

**4. Does at least one other person outside the business know what you are doing and believe in it?**

Not because external validation is required, but because the first year is hard and you will need people who remember why you did this when you temporarily forget.

**5. Can ABLE survive 2 weeks without you?**

The automations are running. The community manager (if hired) is operational. No P0 bugs are open. If you were unreachable for 14 days, the revenue would continue and artists would continue to be onboarded.

If the answer is no — the product is too fragile for you to exit yet. Fix the fragility first.

---

## The things that are true, stated plainly

**You are not behind.** Reaching this point while employed, managing C5/C6, and building largely alone is a significant achievement. The tendency at the exit moment is to compare to a fantasy version of the journey that was linear and fast. That version does not exist.

**The hard part is not behind you.** The first year full-time is genuinely difficult. Not in the way employment is difficult — in a different way. The difficulty is internal: structure, identity, uncertainty, isolation. You have prepared for this. It is still going to be hard. That is normal.

**You built something real.** Paying artists chose to spend money on ABLE. That is the most honest signal that exists in product work. People voted with their money. That is not nothing.

**You can go back.** This is rarely said, but it is true. If in Month 6 the business has stalled, the MRR is declining, and the financial pressure is becoming genuinely dangerous — you can take employment again. It will not feel like going back. It will feel like a deliberate decision with better information. The exit is not a one-way door. The option to return exists, and knowing it exists makes the exit less charged.

**This is the beginning of a different kind of life, not the resolution of a difficult period.** There is no moment where the difficulty ends and the freedom begins. The two coexist permanently. Accepting this makes it sustainable.

---

## The one thing to remember in Month 4

Month 4 is typically the trough. The initial energy of freedom has worn off. The product is not moving as fast as you hoped. You are not sure whether the trajectory is right. Everything feels hard in a dull, grinding way rather than an exciting, intense way.

In Month 4: reread Part 0 of the master strategy document. Read it fully. Then read your weekly log from Months 1 through 4.

You will see movement that is invisible when you are inside it. You will remember why you did this. You will continue.
