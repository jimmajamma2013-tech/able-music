## The path from 3 to 10

### Stage 1: Foundation (3/10 → 5/10)
*Target: complete within 60 days*

These items unlock everything else. Do them first.

1. **Incorporate ABLE Labs Ltd** — £50, 24 hours, Companies House online. This is the legal entity everything else attaches to. Do this week.

2. **Open Starling Business account** — requires Companies House confirmation. Do immediately after incorporation.

3. **Engage an accountant** — search "SaaS startup accountant UK" or ask in Indie Hackers. Budget £1,200/year. Brief them on ABLE's revenue model. Ask them specifically about R&D tax credits and director salary structure.

4. **Calculate emergency fund target** — log into your personal bank, look at average monthly spend over the last 3 months, multiply by 6. That number goes into the Notion dashboard. This is the target.

5. **Open a personal high-interest savings account** — Marcus, Chip, or Chase UK. Start moving money toward the emergency fund target monthly.

6. **Publish privacy policy and terms of service** — use Genie AI templates as a starting point. Get them reviewed. Publish at ablemusic.co/privacy and ablemusic.co/terms. This is a prerequisite for having real users.

### Stage 2: Systems live (5/10 → 7/10)
*Target: complete within 4 months*

7. **Build n8n weekly digest** — the Monday MRR + artist metrics digest. 2 hours to build, weeks of value. Once this is running, you know where you stand without logging into multiple dashboards.

8. **Build n8n pre-shift trigger alert** — the monthly check that fires when MRR > £5k for 3 consecutive months. This turns the trigger from a manual check into an automated signal.

9. **Create Notion pre-shift dashboard** — a single page with all checklist items, financial snapshot, and decision log. This is the weekly review home.

10. **Start the 300-artist spreadsheet** — identify the first 100 artists on TikTok/SoundCloud/Instagram who fit the target profile. This is the top of funnel for ABLE's growth AND James's personal validation of product-market fit.

11. **Income protection insurance** — get 3 quotes. Choose one. This must be in place before the exit.

12. **Active physio relationship** — find a specialist in cervical disc issues. Book an initial consultation. Keep booking maintenance sessions monthly.

## The items that have disproportionate leverage

If forced to pick five items that make the biggest difference:

**1. Emergency fund (personal):** This single item does more for decision quality than anything else. Financial security is the difference between making good decisions and making panicked ones in Month 3.

**2. Income protection insurance:** The C5/C6 condition makes this not optional. One bad flare during Month 4, and without this, the financial situation becomes an emergency simultaneously with the health situation.

**3. Accountant:** Not because accountants are hard to find, but because the first year of director income is genuinely complex (P45, self-assessment, payment on account, director salary structure, R&D credits). Getting this wrong costs real money and causes real stress.

**4. n8n pre-shift trigger alert:** This turns the exit trigger from something James manually checks every month into something the system reports. Automation of the decision signal is itself a signal that the business is operationally sound.

**5. Active physio relationship:** This is the one on the list that has nothing to do with the business and everything to do with whether James can sustain the intensity of full-time founder life. The C5/C6 condition is managed, not cured. Managed means active maintenance. Active maintenance means a physio who knows James, not a new person found in a crisis.
