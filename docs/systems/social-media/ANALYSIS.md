## 1. Platform Presence Audit — Current State

The ACCOUNT-STRATEGY.md audit table has every row marked "Check" — meaning the audit has not been completed. Before any content is created, the audit must be done.

**The audit produces four possible outcomes per platform:**
1. Handle secured, account in good state → update profile and start posting
2. Handle secured, account in poor state → archive old posts, update profile, restart
3. Handle not secured, available → register immediately
4. Handle squatted or unavailable → secure consistent alternative (@ablefm) across all platforms

**The consistent alternative rule matters:** If @able is taken on Instagram (almost certain), the handle must be @ablefm everywhere — not @ablefm on Instagram and @ableartist on TikTok. Inconsistency across handles creates brand confusion and makes it harder for artists to find ABLE after seeing it mentioned elsewhere.

**P0 actions (before any content is posted):**
- Instagram: attempt @able, secure @ablefm if taken
- TikTok: same handle as Instagram — consistency is non-negotiable
- X/Twitter: @ablefm
- Store all credentials in 1Password — one entry per account

**Time cost:** 30–60 minutes to check and secure all P0 handles.

## 2. Content Strategy Quality Assessment

**The 70/20/10 ratio is correct for ABLE's audience:**
- 70% about artists (Pillar 1, 2, 5) — builds trust with the target user before asking them to sign up
- 20% about ABLE as a product (Pillar 3, 4) — demonstrates the product's value without selling
- 10% building-in-public (Pillar 3, behind-the-build) — builds founder credibility and authenticity

**The five content pillars are well-differentiated:**

**Pillar 1 (The Moment):** Emotional reality of independent artist life. Does not require product mentions. Can be created before any artists are on ABLE. This is the pre-launch engine.

**Pillar 2 (The Craft):** Celebrating actual work — production process, release days, venue photos. Requires real artists' content. Activates at 5+ artists.

**Pillar 3 (The Tools):** Practical guidance for independent artists. Does not require ABLE to exist — a post about "how to build a fan email list" is useful regardless of whether ABLE is mentioned.

**Pillar 4 (The Comparison):** ABLE vs. Linktree demonstration. Requires a live ABLE page with real content. This is the highest-leverage single piece of content once available.

**Pillar 5 (The Community):** Featuring real artists. Activates at 10+ artists with their permission.

**What can be created now (pre-artist):** Pillars 1 and 3. Start there. Pillar 4 content (the comparison) should be the first post made once James's own ABLE page is live and genuine.

## 3. Platform-Specific Strategy Assessment

### Instagram — Primary Channel

**The spec is correct about format priority:** Reels > Carousels > Stories > Statics. Reels reach non-followers. That is the only pre-launch acquisition format.

**The four-day posting schedule is achievable but requires content banking.** Posting at 18:00 GMT Tuesday, 12:30 GMT Thursday, 10:00 GMT Saturday, 16:00 GMT Sunday is operationally difficult without 2 weeks of content prepared in advance. The content bank (14 days forward) is not optional — it is the difference between sustainable content and burnout-driven gaps.

**The Instagram bio copy is production-ready.** The version in ACCOUNT-STRATEGY.md ("Your music. Your fans. Your relationship. / No algorithm in the way. / ablemusic.co ↓") is strong. Use it now.

**Highlight strategy needs artists:** The "Artist pages" and "The numbers" highlights cannot be built pre-launch. The "How it works" and "The build" highlights can be started immediately.

### TikTok — Parallel Channel

**The hook-first principle is non-negotiable on TikTok.** The spec gives good examples: "Linktree takes 9% of your merch revenue and doesn't tell you this." This is the correct format — counterintuitive statement before any context.

**The algorithm timing advantage is real:** TikTok's algorithm is more forgiving for new accounts than Instagram. The first 30 days of posting on TikTok are disproportionately high-leverage for organic reach. This window exists right now. Every week of delay closes a small part of that window.

**The key TikTok risk:** voice drift. TikTok rewards fast editing, trending audio, and meme formats — all of which can pull ABLE's voice toward generic creator content. The rule in the spec ("if the audio requires changing what the video communicates, use original audio") is the right constraint. Enforce it.

### X/Twitter — Industry Channel

**The spec correctly identifies X/Twitter as a B2B channel** (music industry: journalists, managers, A&R) rather than a direct artist acquisition channel. This distinction matters for content: X posts can be more analytical and industry-facing than Instagram content.

**The reduced cadence (2–3 posts/week vs. 4–5 on Instagram) is correct.** X/Twitter's organic reach for small accounts is significantly lower than Instagram or TikTok. Invest effort proportionally.

**The building-in-public posts work well on X.** "We shipped [X] today and learned [Y]" performs disproportionately well with the tech-adjacent music industry audience on X. This is one of the few places where product announcements feel natural.

### Discord — Community First

**The spec correctly positions Discord as a space, not a broadcast channel.** The distinction matters. Broadcasting to Discord makes it feel like an email list. Creating a genuine community space makes it the product's most valuable retention mechanism.

**Do not open Discord until there are 20 artists to seed it with.** An empty Discord server with James as the only member sends the wrong signal to new artists. The spec has this right. Wait for the critical mass, then open it.

## 4. Dead Account Protocol Assessment

The three-scenario protocol (abandoned own account, squatted handle, off-brand historical content) is operationally complete.

**The archive-over-delete principle is correct:** archiving preserves history and is reversible; deletion is permanent and removes authentic brand history. ABLE's early imperfect posts are honest. They are not a liability unless they actively contradict current positioning.

**The quarterly dormant account review (10 minutes per account)** is the correct maintenance cadence. The most important checks are: account not compromised, profile photo current, bio current, redirect post pinned. 10 minutes per account per quarter is a minimal commitment that prevents brand damage.

**Password rotation every six months** is appropriate for social accounts. Use a password manager (1Password per the spec) and unique passwords per account. Never reuse.

## 5. Content Sustainability Assessment

The spec achieves a high level of specificity for Week 1. Weeks 2–4 are described thematically. This gap (the FINAL-REVIEW's conditional pass on Gate 10) is the most important sustainability question for a solo founder.

**The honest sustainability ceiling for James alone:**
- 2 Reels/week: achievable with a 2-week content bank
- 1 Carousel/week: achievable
- 5 Stories/week: achievable (low production effort)
- Weekly scheduling check: 30 minutes
- **Total weekly content time: approximately 4–6 hours**

This is manageable alongside product development if content is batched (one afternoon per week for all content), not created daily. The spec's "2 quality beats 4 rushed ones" rule is the correct guideline — if a week is a heavy development week, post 2 quality Reels rather than 4 mediocre ones.

**The content bank structure:** A rolling 2-week bank of fully produced content, scheduled in Buffer or Later. When the bank drops below 1 week, scheduling takes priority over other tasks. This prevents the "I have nothing to post today" problem.

**The risk of scheduled content:** A pre-scheduled post about Linktree going up on the same day Linktree announces a relevant feature is awkward. Set up a 24-hour review before any scheduled post publishes. Not automatic publishing — scheduled drafts that James approves the morning of posting.

## 6. Producer Seeding — Most Important Non-Post Activity

The SOCIAL-MEDIA-PLAN.md notes this in the launch plan and it is also identified in the organic growth and marketing analyses as the highest-ROI pre-launch activity.

**Why producer seeding is social media strategy:** Every DM to a UK producer is social media outreach. It happens off the public feed but it drives the public growth engine.

**The urgency argument in the spec is correct:** "Producer relationships feel most natural immediately. The longer you wait after deciding to do this, the more awkward a cold outreach becomes." This is a window that opens once. Use it in the first week.

**The target:** 20 UK producers who work with 3–10 artists each. One producer who joins and mentions ABLE to their artists = 3–10 artist sign-ups with pre-existing trust. No Instagram Reel achieves this ROI.

**The practical action today:** Write a list of 20 UK producers (not megastars — working producers with modest followings who are in the target artist demographic). DM the first 5 this week. This requires no content creation and no audience.

## 7. Artist Spotlight Series

The artist spotlight series (Pillar 5 in the plan) is a key retention and growth mechanism for the social channels once artists are on the platform.

**The value exchange is correctly specified:** ABLE features the artist, drives traffic back to their ABLE page, shows them a spike in profile views. The artist gets something real; ABLE gets authentic content and a brand advocate.

**The DM template in the spec is strong.** The key elements: reference something specific about the artist's work (proves it is not a mass DM), make the ask small (we want to feature you, not much required), give them editorial control.

**The first 10 artist spotlights are the most important.** They set the quality standard for the series, they are the social proof that other artists see when deciding whether to agree to a feature, and they are the content bank that validates Pillar 5 as a sustainable format.

**Do not start artist spotlights until there are 20+ artists on the platform.** Before that, Pillar 1 and Pillar 3 content are the pre-launch engine.

## 9. Key Risks

**Risk 1: Voice drift under posting pressure.**
The ABLE voice is strong in the spec but fragile in execution. One rushed post that sounds like a SaaS company — even a minor slip toward "grow your audience" territory — damages the account's credibility with the target audience. The mitigation: read every post out loud before scheduling. If it would make an independent artist wince, rewrite it.

**Risk 2: Instagram algorithm requiring consistency.**
Instagram's 2025–2026 algorithm weights account consistency heavily. Posting 4 times in one week and 1 time the next creates momentum loss. The 2-week content bank is the structural solution.

**Risk 3: Producer seeding not started.**
This is the highest-leverage pre-launch activity and the one most likely to be deferred because it feels like it can wait. It cannot. The window for natural outreach is open now. Start this week.

**Risk 4: Comparison content (Pillar 4) delayed.**
The most powerful piece of content ABLE can create is a screen recording comparing an ABLE page to a Linktree page on release day. This requires James's own ABLE page to be genuinely live with real music. Until it is, the most compelling content in the arsenal is unavailable. Make the personal page priority.
