### 18. RED TEAM (What would make this strategy fail?)
*Score: n/a — threat analysis*

**Threat 1: James doesn't post consistently.** The strategy requires 4 posts/week minimum, plus daily comment engagement. If that cadence isn't maintained, the algorithm deprioritises the account and the audience doesn't develop. Mitigation: Buffer pre-scheduled posts for the week every Monday morning. One 2-hour session sets the week.

**Threat 2: The building-in-public content starts to feel performative.** There's a version of "honest building-in-public" that is actually a sophisticated form of marketing, and audiences eventually detect it. Mitigation: The "authenticity ceiling" section addresses this. The test is: would James post this if no one read it? If not, don't post it.

**Threat 3: Artist spotlights become a liability.** If a featured artist has a public controversy after being featured, their content stays in ABLE's highlights. Mitigation: Archive spotlights from Highlights after 6 months. Never promise permanent features.

**Threat 4: Competitor comparison content is used against ABLE.** If ABLE's pricing changes or a competitor changes their fees, old comparison posts become inaccurate. Mitigation: Quarterly review of all comparison content. Add a source date to all fee-related posts.

**Threat 5: The account grows without converting.** Large Instagram followings that don't drive signups are a vanity trap. Mitigation: Part 8's metrics explicitly prioritise profile link clicks and waitlist conversions over follower count. Check these weekly, not just monthly.

## THE 5 HIGHEST-IMPACT CHANGES

1. **Add a baseline acknowledgement to the metrics section** — targets as written assume account momentum; add a "0–500 follower" phase with different benchmarks to prevent early discouragement.

2. **Add a "rolling Week 2" template** — the Week 1 calendar is complete, but without a template for Week 2 the strategy requires significant creative output every Sunday night. A reusable weekly structure reduces that friction to almost zero.

3. **Add a one-page quick reference card for brand voice** — the condensed version in Part 10 is useful but still 200+ words. A 50-word card (the five rules, the banned words) is what a community manager needs at their desk.

4. **Add a milestone-triggered content guide** — when ABLE hits 50 artists / Music Ally coverage / £1,000 MRR, what specific social content gets posted? Social should amplify milestones, not just report them in the monthly reflection.

5. **Quarterly hashtag review reminder** — hashtag performance shifts. A recurring review task (calendar reminder) to update the hashtag lists in Part 7 would keep the strategy current without requiring a full document revision.
