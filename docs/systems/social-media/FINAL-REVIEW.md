## Quality gates passed

### Gate 1: Monday morning usability
**Pass.** Part 3 (Week 1 Calendar) contains 7 days of fully written posts — captions, hashtags, visual direction, timing, and CTAs. A person unfamiliar with ABLE's strategy could execute this without reading anything else.

### Gate 2: Copy philosophy compliance
**Pass.** Every example post checked against `docs/v6/core/COPY_AND_DESIGN_PHILOSOPHY.md`:
- No exclamation marks in any caption.
- No banned words (grow, convert, monetise, superfan, content creator, going viral, get started, unlock, leverage).
- No generic SaaS micro-copy.
- All 35+ example posts tested. Zero violations found.

### Gate 3: Artist-first framing
**Pass.** The Artist Spotlight series, education pillar, and community pillar are structured to benefit artists independently of ABLE. Education posts (Pillar 4) explicitly don't require product mentions. The spotlight DM templates are written to give the featured artist editorial control.

### Gate 4: Honesty and authenticity ceiling
**Pass.** Part 6 (Building-in-public Strategy) explicitly prohibits fabricated metrics, manufactured urgency, and selective social proof. Part 9 (Crisis Management) gives a practical framework for handling criticism. The comparison posts acknowledge ABLE's limitations.

### Gate 5: Platform differentiation
**Pass.** Instagram, TikTok, X, YouTube, and Reddit each have distinct strategies. The Instagram/TikTok distinction is particularly well-drawn — the "same story, different telling" principle prevents content recycling while maintaining strategic coherence.

### Gate 6: Pre-launch viability
**Pass.** Weeks 1–4 of Part 4 are specific enough to execute before any artists are on the platform. Week 1 has zero product mentions — all posts are genuinely useful independent content.

### Gate 7: Outsourcing readiness
**Pass.** Part 10 defines clearly what a community manager can post without approval vs what requires James. The brand voice training section is concise and testable.

### Gate 8: Metrics are honest
**Pass.** All targets cited have a basis in music industry benchmarks, not invented aspiration. The qualitative signals section (Part 8) correctly identifies leading indicators that matter more than follower count.

## Quality gates with conditions

### Gate 9: Metrics baseline
**Conditional pass.** The targets in Part 8 assume some account momentum. The gap between 0 followers and 500 followers is not addressed. Recommend adding a Phase 0 note acknowledging this. Functionality: high — the strategy works without it, but early-stage discouragement is a real risk.

### Gate 10: Week 2+ content sustainability
**Conditional pass.** The Week 1 calendar is complete. Weeks 2–4 are described thematically in Part 4 but without the same level of specificity. The rolling week template recommended in PATH-TO-10 would close this gap.

## What this document does well

**The specificity is real.** Most social media plans contain principles without examples. This document has 35+ fully written posts across 7 content pillars — posts that could be published without editing. This is the difference between a strategy document and an execution document.

**The copy register holds throughout.** 10,000+ words of example copy and every piece holds to the ABLE voice. The building-in-public pillar in particular sounds genuinely like a thoughtful founder talking about the product — not like a brand account performing authenticity.

**The comparison content is handled correctly.** Acknowledging Linktree's strengths in the comparison pillar is an unusual and smart choice. It will earn more trust than any number of negative feature comparisons.

**The artist spotlight series has genuine value exchange.** The DM templates, the editorial review step, the follow-up process showing traffic spikes back to the artist — this is a partnership, not an extraction. Artists will agree to this because they get something real.

**The authenticity ceiling section is the most important part.** The temptation to post fabricated social proof or inflated metrics is real at the pre-launch stage. The explicit list of what never to post is the most important quality gate in the document.
