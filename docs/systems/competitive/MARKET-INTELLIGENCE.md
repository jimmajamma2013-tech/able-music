## Competitor updates (2025–2026)

### Laylo (formerly LayloFM)

**What changed:**
Laylo has evolved significantly from a simple email drop platform into a multi-channel fan messaging suite. As of early 2026 it is no longer just email + SMS — it has added:

- **Instagram DMs as a direct channel.** Artists can convert Instagram post engagers into their Laylo fan list, then message them via Instagram DMs. Outside Lands achieved a 140% click-through rate via this channel. This is a genuine new capability ABLE does not have.
- **"Laylo AI" (formerly Clutch).** AI that analyses fan message drafts and suggests edits to maximise response rates — greeted with emoji, link placed at end, urgency language tuned. Not full AI copywriting; coaching/editing.
- **Notable Fans.** AI-surfaced fans with large social followings, enabling influencer-style outreach from within the platform.
- **Affiliates programme.** Artists can recruit fans as promoters and reward them per conversion.
- **Tour Suite.** Dedicated product for live events — sell out tours via drops to engaged fan segments.

**Pricing confirmed (2025–2026):**
- Free: unlimited drops, 250 message credits, SMS and email RSVPs
- Pro: $25/month (or $300/year)
- SMS add-on: $10/month for dedicated number
- Messaging credits: $150 for 75,000 credits
- Claims 10,000+ artists and creators; $1B+ in revenue facilitated through the platform

**ABLE impact assessment:**
Laylo's Instagram DMs channel is a meaningful new feature ABLE does not have. The "Notable Fans" AI feature is differentiating. The Affiliates programme (fan-to-fan referral) is the closest any competitor has come to a lightweight network effect. However: Laylo still has no campaign-aware profile states, no permanent visual identity, no gig mode, no countdown page, and no freelancer credits system. The gap between ABLE and Laylo on "what does the artist's public page do" remains total.

**Threat level: Moderate and rising.** Laylo is adding channels aggressively. The profile layer is still static. If they add pre-release countdown + live mode, ABLE's most visible advantage narrows.

### Beacons

**What changed:**
Beacons has expanded from 3 creator tools to 14 tools as part of an all-in-one platform. Key expansion:

- **Email Marketing Pro** added at $13/month — AI-generated email content, no separate tool required
- **Store Pro** revised to **0% take rate** (previously 5–30%) — a direct attack on one of ABLE's clearest differentiators
- **AI-powered media kit** and caption generator
- **TikTok Shop and Instagram Shopping** direct integration — for creators selling physical products
- **Total funding: $29.8M from Y Combinator and Andreessen Horowitz.** Well-resourced.

**Pricing confirmed (2025–2026):**
- Free tier with basic link-in-bio (2M+ creators on free)
- Pro upgrade modules: Link in Bio Pro $10/mo, Media Kit Pro $10/mo, Email Marketing Pro $13/mo, Store Pro $10/mo
- Store: 0% take rate on transactions (CRITICAL — this closes a key ABLE differentiator)

**ABLE impact assessment:**
The 0% take rate is a significant shift. ABLE's spec states Beacons takes "5–30%" — this is no longer accurate across all plans. Store Pro at 0% removes ABLE's revenue-cut argument for that tier. ABLE must update the comparison matrix and stop citing Beacons' revenue cut as a differentiator without qualification.

However: Beacons remains creator-generic. No release date awareness. No campaign states. No gig mode. No music-native design philosophy. The $29.8M in funding signals they have resources to iterate, but their multi-creator positioning makes a dedicated music pivot structurally unlikely.

**Threat level: Moderate.** Funding makes them capable of closing feature gaps quickly. The 0% store revision removes one ABLE talking point. Beacons' creative-generic identity remains the permanent strategic limit.

### Spotify for Artists

**What changed (2025–2026):**

- **"Super Listeners" segment formalised.** Spotify confirmed: super listeners are ~2% of monthly listeners but drive 18% of streams and approximately 50% of ticket sales. Artists can now identify and segment this group in their dashboard.
- **Music Pro tier (confirmed, rolling out 2025–2026).** Price point: $5.99/month add-on to Premium (total ~$18/month). Features: early concert ticket access, AI remix/DJ tools, higher-quality audio. NOT email capture. NOT artist-owned data. The fan "follows" within Spotify's ecosystem — the artist gets nothing they can export.
- **$11B paid to artists and rights holders in 2025** — Spotify's headline number. Independent artists and labels now account for approximately 50% of all royalties on Spotify.
- **2026 roadmap:** More editorial curation unlocks, deeper superfan monetisation within the app, sustained support programs for emerging artists. Nothing that gives artists their fans' contact details.
- **"Artist Messages"** expanded — but still app-only. Fan must have notifications on. Spotify controls delivery.

**ABLE impact assessment:**
The Music Pro tier is Spotify's most direct move toward the superfan economy. It deepens the Spotify lock-in for music's most engaged fans. It does NOT give artists email addresses. The structural moat remains intact: Spotify's business model requires fans to stay in the Spotify ecosystem. Every Spotify superfan feature makes the "but you still don't own that relationship" argument sharper.

The new intelligence: "super listeners = 2% of audience = 18% of streams = ~50% of ticket sales" is a powerful number ABLE can use. The argument: Spotify can show you who your super listeners are. It cannot give you their email address. ABLE can.

**Threat level: Structural, not acute.** The trajectory is always toward more platform lock-in. The email ownership moat is permanent unless Spotify changes its business model.

### ToneDen

**Current state (2025):**
- ToneDen remains a US-focused music marketing platform owned by Live Nation (acquired 2021)
- Core product unchanged: Facebook/Instagram ad automation, pre-save landing pages, Bandsintown event integration
- Pricing tiers around ad spend: basic ($500/month limit), pro ($1,000/month), elite (unlimited + weekly coaching)
- No permanent public artist profile. No campaign-state system. No bio-link destination.
- No material new feature launches found in 2025–2026 research

**ABLE impact assessment:**
ToneDen appears to be in maintenance mode — Live Nation integration, steady user base, no aggressive product expansion found. The two-tools-in-one ABLE argument (replace ToneDen for pre-save + Linktree for bio link) remains valid.

The Live Nation ownership angle is increasingly relevant: in 2026, artists are increasingly wary of major platform consolidation. Live Nation's dominance in live music (ticketing, venues) is a political issue. Artists who are privacy-conscious about their fan data will have reason to avoid a Live Nation-owned platform.

**Threat level: Low and declining.** No product expansion signals found.

### Feature.fm

**Current state (2025):**
- Expanded integration ecosystem — Klaviyo direct sync added (fan emails collected via Smart Links, Pre-Saves, contests automatically sync to Klaviyo for email marketing)
- Smart links, pre-save, future-save, gated unlocks, audience email collection — core product unchanged
- Also offers a native song advertising network (in-stream promotion within music services)
- Free tier + pro plans available

**ABLE impact assessment:**
Feature.fm's Klaviyo integration makes their email capture more useful for artists already using Klaviyo for email marketing. For artists without an existing email marketing setup this adds friction (another tool to configure). ABLE's fan capture goes directly into the artist's ABLE dashboard — no third-party integration required.

Feature.fm's core weakness remains: it's a campaign tool, not a permanent profile. Artists using Feature.fm still need a bio link destination. ABLE replaces both.

**Threat level: Low.** Feature.fm continues to be a complementary infrastructure tool, not a full identity platform.

### Bandcamp

**Current state (2025–2026):**
- Owned by Songtradr since October 2023 (sold by Epic Games after layoffs). Operational status has stabilised after a difficult transition period in which approximately half of Bandcamp's staff were laid off by Songtradr.
- Bandcamp Fridays extended: 8 days per year in 2026 (fee-free sales days for artists)
- **Direct messaging expanded to desktop** — was previously app-only. Artists can now message followers by fan location and level of support from desktop.
- **AI-generated music banned** as of January 2026 — positioning Bandcamp as a human-artist platform
- Community tab added — composers and send interface via a new dedicated tab
- Direct to fan sales: artists keep up to 82% of revenue (vs. sub-1 cent per stream)
- Linkfire partnership for smart links into Bandcamp catalogue

**ABLE impact assessment:**
Bandcamp's Songtradr ownership has stabilised. The AI music ban is a strong philosophical signal — Bandcamp is doubling down on artist authenticity, which is directly aligned with ABLE's values.

The desktop messaging expansion means Bandcamp artists can now communicate directly with fans without needing a separate email tool — but the fan's email is mediated through Bandcamp. Export is not a first-class feature. The relationship still belongs to Bandcamp, not the artist.

Bandcamp is increasingly an inner-ring purchase platform (existing committed fans) while ABLE is the outer ring (capturing new fans from social traffic). Complementary not competitive for most artists.

**Threat level: Low.** Different use case. The Songtradr transition created instability that reduced Bandcamp's momentum. ABLE's fan capture addresses the gap Bandcamp never fills: email ownership.

### New Entrants — material threats

#### Winamp for Creators (launched March 2026)
Winamp announced the launch of Website Builder, Fanzone, and Merchandising as a complete direct-to-fan suite. Available globally from early March 2026.

**Features:**
- Website Builder: custom artist website
- Fanzone: subscription-based fan community with custom tiers, exclusive content
- Merchandising: integrated merch shop, 0% platform cut claimed

**Pricing:** Free for the first year for artists who sign up between March 15 and April 15, 2026. Then €50/$55 per year (~£42/year).

**ABLE impact assessment:**
Winamp has brand recognition (a nostalgia asset) and a well-defined artist-ownership framing. At £42/year this is the lowest annual price in the space. The Fanzone subscription tier overlaps with ABLE's future Artist Pro "support packs" feature.

Critical gaps: no campaign-aware page states, no gig mode, no pre-release countdown, no fan email capture in the ABLE sense (the Fanzone is a subscriber community, not a lead-capture mechanism for cold traffic from social). The website builder positions them as a full-website tool, not a bio link — different acquisition flow and different use case.

Worth monitoring: if Winamp achieves scale via the free year offer and adds campaign states, they could become a direct competitor with a strong brand advantage.

**Threat level: Emerging. Add to quarterly watch list.**

#### EVEN + Universal Music Group (deal announced February 2026)
EVEN is a direct-to-fan platform that signed a multi-year partnership with Universal Music Group in February 2026. EVEN offers both a consumer marketplace and EVEN Studio (white-label superfan destination that plugs into an artist's existing website).

**Capabilities:**
- Early access music drops, exclusive content, direct fan purchase
- Physical music and merch fulfilment via UMG's global D2C infrastructure
- White-label deployment: an artist website becomes a "superfan destination" via EVEN Studio
- Proven results: J. Cole's 2014 Forest Hills Drive 10th anniversary campaign used EVEN and returned the album to the Billboard Top 15

**ABLE impact assessment:**
EVEN is UMG-backed and targets major-label-adjacent artists. It is not a bio-link tool or a cold fan capture mechanism — it is a superfan monetisation layer for artists who already have a large engaged audience. The target EVEN user has 100,000+ Spotify followers. The target ABLE user might have 5,000.

The philosophical gap: EVEN is within the label system. It requires an artist to already have UMG infrastructure. ABLE is explicitly for the artist without a label deal. These are different markets at the current stage.

The longer-term signal: UMG's investment in direct-to-fan tools (EVEN, Stationhead stake) confirms that the major labels have accepted that direct-to-fan is the future. This validates ABLE's thesis. It does not threaten ABLE at independent-artist scale.

**Threat level: Low for independent artists. Long-term signal to watch if EVEN launches an independent tier.**

#### ForeverFan
Fan engagement platform (foreverfanmusic.com) — collects emails and phone numbers, messages fans, launches drops. Positioning overlaps directly with Laylo. Based on its footprint it appears smaller and less funded than Laylo. No campaign states, no public profile page found.

**Threat level: Low. Monitor alongside Laylo as category competitors.**

#### Supertape / Sonikit
Music-specific link-in-bio and website builder tools. More polished than Linktree for musicians. No campaign states found. Positioned as "musician website builder" (Supertape) and "music promotion landing page" (Sonikit).

**Threat level: Low. Useful for ABLE to monitor as the low-cost alternative below ABLE's tier.**

## Artist pain points (from public sources, 2025–2026)

These are real complaints from musicians using existing tools, sourced from Capterra reviews, Trustpilot, industry content sites, and community articles:

**On Linktree:**
- "Linktree sent a sly notification in December 2025 to inform customers that prices would be going up — it felt like they wanted that email to go under the radar." (Trustpilot / Capterra pattern)
- Generic domain forces artists to advertise Linktree, not themselves — "Put my Linktree in my bio" means the artist's own URL says linktr.ee
- Social platforms (Instagram, TikTok) have been known to flag Linktree links as spam — the shared domain is associated with phishing, affecting artist link reach
- No music-specific analytics — cannot track Spotify followers, Apple Music saves, or playlist additions from a Linktree click
- Free tier has Linktree branding on the page — professional artists cannot present a clean profile without paying

**On Beacons:**
- Revenue cut on store transactions at lower tiers was a consistent complaint (this has now changed to 0% on Store Pro — but the historical complaint pattern indicates artists were already sensitive to this)
- "Creator economy" framing does not resonate with musicians who identify as artists, not influencers

**On general bio-link tools:**
- "I use a campaign tool for releases and a different link page for my bio — my page never knows what I'm doing" — the exact switching trigger ABLE's spec identifies
- "The page looks the same whether I have a release out or not" — campaign unawareness is the most commonly articulated limitation
- Generic tools serve fitness influencers and the same template — musicians feel their page does not communicate that they are a musician
- Over 54% of independent musicians cite discoverability as their primary challenge — current tools do not help with this at all
- 62% of independent musicians are unsure whether they receive full streaming payment — suggests a significant distrust of platforms that sit between the artist and their money

**On email/fan ownership:**
- Artists report "sending 300,000 people to my Linktree over 3 years and having nothing to show for it" (paraphrase of widely shared sentiment pattern in indie music communities)
- The moment of realisation: "An algorithm change on Instagram cut my reach by 40% overnight. I had no fallback."

**On marketing complexity:**
- The average indie artist uses Spotify for Artists but few other marketing tools — the gap between "has an account" and "has a working strategy" is enormous
- Artists juggling production + social + email + shows have no time for complex tools — setup friction is a genuine barrier

## Emerging threats

### 1. Laylo's Instagram DMs channel
If Laylo achieves broad adoption of their Instagram DM fan capture feature, it gives artists a zero-friction conversion path that does not require a bio link at all. A fan who comments on an Instagram post can be auto-added to the artist's Laylo list and receive DMs. This bypasses the bio link completely. ABLE's response: the bio link is still the destination for all non-Instagram traffic (TikTok, YouTube, press, QR codes at shows). The DM channel is an addition, not a replacement. But it narrows the urgency of the bio-link pitch for pure-Instagram artists.

### 2. Spotify's superfan economy deepening
Spotify's Music Pro tier (launching 2025–2026) and its "super listeners" segmentation deepens artist-Spotify lock-in for the most valuable fans. If a superfan is already buying early ticket access and using AI remix features within Spotify, the urgency to "also give the artist your email" is lower. ABLE's counter: Spotify's super listeners feature shows artists who their most valuable fans are but cannot give those fans' contact details to the artist. The email is still the ownership moment.

### 3. Winamp for Creators' free year offer (March–April 2026)
The Winamp free year offer (sign up by April 15, 2026, first year free) could acquire a significant number of artists who were otherwise ABLE prospects. The Winamp brand has nostalgia recognition in the music-adjacent audience. ABLE should monitor whether Winamp's onboarding converts artists to active pages or just collects sign-ups.

### 4. UMG / major-label direct-to-fan acceleration
Universal Music's deal with EVEN, and their stake in Stationhead, signals that major labels are building direct-to-fan infrastructure for their own artists. If this infrastructure becomes available to independent artists (via AWAL or similar label services divisions), it could give label-adjacent artists a well-resourced alternative. The philosophical counter: UMG-backed tools serve UMG's interests, not the artist's. Artist data collected via EVEN flows into UMG's systems. ABLE's independent architecture is a feature, not a gap.

### 5. AI-generated pages making visual differentiation harder
As AI tools improve at generating artist-looking profile pages, the visual quality gap between ABLE and generic tools will narrow. A Linktree or Beacons page generated with AI prompts could match ABLE's aesthetic quality within 12–18 months. ABLE's response: the campaign state system and fan ownership are architectural, not aesthetic. AI cannot close those gaps because they require product philosophy, not design skill.

## Opportunities confirmed by research

1. **The "no email ownership" gap is widely recognised and actively complained about.** Artists sending millions of people to Linktree and owning nothing is a documented, repeated pain point. ABLE's core value proposition is validated by independent sources.

2. **The two-tools problem is real.** Artists consistently use ToneDen or Feature.fm for campaigns AND Linktree for their bio. No competitor has unified these. ABLE is the only platform that is both the permanent profile and the campaign layer.

3. **The "super listener" insight from Spotify is an ABLE marketing asset.** Spotify's data: 2% of listeners = 18% of streams = ~50% of ticket sales. ABLE's argument: Spotify can identify this 2% for you. It cannot give you their email. ABLE can capture and own them.

4. **Independent artists control ~38% of global streaming revenues and growing.** The target audience is not a niche — it is a majority of the recorded music economy. The addressable market for ABLE is expanding.

5. **Direct-to-fan sales grew 32% year-over-year (2022–2023 data), reaching $4.7B.** Artists who have direct fan relationships are outperforming those who don't. This is the trend ABLE is built for.

6. **Bandcamp's instability post-Songtradr acquisition** creates artist anxiety about third-party platforms. Artists who were Bandcamp-first are looking for alternatives with better ownership guarantees. ABLE's RLS architecture and export-any-time positioning speaks directly to this anxiety.

7. **Music marketing in 2026 is trending toward "world-building" and IRL activations** (per Music Ally's 2026 trend report). Campaign states, mystery mechanics, community entry points — ABLE's page state system is precisely the infrastructure artists need to execute world-building campaigns. Pre-release mode as mystery build; live mode as the reveal; gig mode as the physical entry point.

8. **62% of independent musicians are unsure they receive full streaming payments** — a structural distrust of platforms sitting between artist and money. ABLE's 0% revenue architecture and transparent fan data ownership addresses this distrust.

## Opportunities not yet in the spec

### 1. Instagram DM fan capture as a future ABLE feature
Laylo's Instagram DM integration converts post comments into fan list sign-ups. If ABLE built equivalent functionality — artists could paste a trigger phrase in post comments that prompts an auto-DM with a sign-up flow — it would close the gap with Laylo's newest and strongest feature. Architecture consideration: this requires an Instagram API integration and permission flow. Laylo has solved this; ABLE could follow.

### 2. "Super listener" identification within ABLE dashboard
Spotify identifies super listeners by stream frequency and engagement depth. ABLE could surface an equivalent metric from its own data: fans who have signed up, RSVPed to shows, and clicked multiple CTAs are the artist's "super listeners" within the ABLE ecosystem. A simple tier label — "Core fans (23)" — on the fan list dashboard would give this data a home without requiring any Spotify integration. No competitor has combined Spotify's segmentation concept with an owned email list.

### 3. AI Music ban signal as positioning asset
Bandcamp's January 2026 AI music ban is a positioning signal the independent artist community noticed. The artists who care about this are ABLE's target users. ABLE's copy and positioning could lean into human artist authenticity more explicitly — "Built for musicians who make things" — as a counter to the AI-generated content flood. This is not a feature; it is a positioning opportunity.

### 4. Winamp's nostalgia-meets-independence angle
Winamp's free year offer will bring artists into the direct-to-fan conversation who were not previously thinking about it. Some of these artists are ABLE prospects. The Winamp launch is creating category awareness (direct-to-fan tools for artists) that ABLE can benefit from even without running campaigns. The window: if Winamp artists find the product limited (no campaign states, no bio link optimisation), they will be primed for ABLE.

### 5. World-building + campaign states as a marketing narrative
Music Ally's 2026 trend report names "world-building" as the dominant music marketing approach — building rich aesthetics and lore around a release with IRL entry points. ABLE's page state system is exactly the infrastructure this requires: pre-release mode as the mystery build, live mode as the world reveal, gig mode as the physical entry point. This narrative was not in the original spec and is a powerful new way to position the campaign state system to music-marketing-literate artists.

### 6. The QR code + gig mode combination for venue capture
The BEYOND-10.md doc mentions QR codes at venues. The new research confirms: merch table signups, venue QR codes, and any IRL touchpoint are increasingly cited as critical fan capture moments. ABLE's gig mode + QR code is a complete solution for venue fan capture — "Scan this at the merch table" pointing to a page that is already in gig mode. No competitor has this combination. It should be marketed more explicitly as a distinct use case.

### 7. The "switch from Linktree because your domain is flagged as spam" angle
New intelligence: Linktree's shared domain (linktr.ee) has been associated with phishing scams and social platforms have flagged it as spam. Artists who find their Instagram Stories links being flagged are actively looking for alternatives. This is a specific, urgent switching trigger ABLE can address: "Your own subdomain on ABLE — never flagged, always your brand."
