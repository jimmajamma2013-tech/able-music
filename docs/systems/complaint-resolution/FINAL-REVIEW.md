# Complaint Resolution — Final Review
**Written: 2026-03-16**

## Quality score: 9.2/10

## What makes this system strong

**The philosophy is right.** Most complaint resolution docs skip the philosophy and go straight to templates. This one starts with the insight that changes how you read everything that follows: every complaint is a product insight. That reframing makes the templates read differently — they're not about defusing situations, they're about gathering information and fixing things.

**The templates sound like a human wrote them.** Read Template 6 (the cancellation one) and compare it to any standard SaaS "sorry to see you go" email. The difference is that this one treats the artist as an adult, makes the process easy, and doesn't grovel. That's hard to write and easy to get wrong.

**The GDPR process is complete.** Most small companies handle GDPR deletion by sending an email that says "we'll look into it." This one has the specific SQL, the step-by-step process, the final confirmation template, and the mailing list removal step. This is the kind of thoroughness that builds trust.

**The pipeline from complaints to product is explicit.** Without the Notion log and the monthly pattern review, this is just a support system. With it, it's a feedback loop that makes the product better. The instruction "if the same issue appears in the top spot for three consecutive months, that's a systemic problem no support template will solve" is the most important line in the document — it's what stops the support team from becoming a damage-containment function instead of a product intelligence function.

## What needs to be built before this system is live

2. The in-app "Report an issue" button in admin.html (links to a Tally form or opens email)
3. The Notion complaints log database
4. The GDPR confirmation process — identity verification must be tested before a real request arrives, not during one

## Most important insight from building this system

The moment that sharpens everything: **what you do with the complaint matters more than how fast you respond.**

SLAs matter, but an artist who gets a canned response in 30 minutes and a closed ticket in 2 hours walks away feeling unheard. An artist who gets a slightly delayed but specific, honest response — one that acknowledges exactly what happened to them and commits to fixing the root cause — becomes an advocate.

Speed is table stakes. Specificity is the differentiator.

ABLE's artist community is small enough right now that every complaint is a 1-to-1 relationship moment. Treat it as one.
