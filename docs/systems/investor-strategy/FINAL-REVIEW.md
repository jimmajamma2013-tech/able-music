## What this document does well

**1. It treats James as a thinking adult, not a pitch machine.**
The document doesn't say "here's how to hype your product." It says "here's what investors actually want to know, here are the honest answers, here's what to avoid." That's more useful than a script.

**2. It is honest about the traction gap.**
Section 1, Question 6: "Current state (honest): ABLE is at the pre-revenue stage." This is the most important paragraph in the document. Founders who try to spin pre-traction into a narrative investors see through it. James should keep this honesty as a value, not hedge it when traction arrives.

**3. The data moat section is the best thing here.**
If James remembers nothing else, remember this: the data moat is the investment thesis. Not the features. Not the design. The fact that ABLE accumulates fan-source attribution data that no streaming platform or distributor has — that is the moat. Use it in every conversation.

**4. The investor archetype section prevents bad deals.**
The five-criteria checklist (Do they understand music? Can they intro 5 artists? Are they comfortable with the 5–7 year timeline?) filters out the investors who would be a liability rather than an asset. Most pitch prep documents don't include this.

The deck outline is complete. The deck itself needs to exist as a file (Notion, Pitch, or Google Slides). This is a 2-hour task once the product has screenshots. It should exist by Month 3.

**2. Populate the 15-person pre-raise list.**
This is the most important pending action. The document tells James to build the list. The list doesn't exist. Start with five names this week: three music industry angels (ex-label, ex-distributor, ex-manager), one creator economy investor, one UK tech angel. Research them. Follow them. Add them to a private note with context on each.

**3. Reflect on the most recent competitive landscape.**
The competitor analysis (Linktree, Beacons, Feature.fm) is accurate as of March 2026 but should be refreshed quarterly. Set a calendar reminder.

## The most important things to remember before any investor conversation

1. Lead with the data moat, not the features.
2. The business model is the differentiator: SaaS, 0% cut, artist-owned data.
3. The market is large, the timing is right, and the tools that exist are genuinely bad.
4. Do not raise before 100 paying artists. The equity isn't worth giving up before that.
5. The one sentence: "Every other platform knows what fans listen to. ABLE knows which fans chose to show up."
