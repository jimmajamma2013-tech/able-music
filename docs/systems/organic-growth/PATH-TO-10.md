### 18. AI RED TEAM (What would kill this system's effectiveness?)

**Threat 1: Impatience in Week 3**
The temptation to skip the earned trust phase and pitch Reddit before Week 5 would get ABLE banned from the most important community channels. The rule "build karma first" is the kill switch for this threat.

**Threat 2: Producer seeding with a template**
If the producer outreach is sent as a template, response rates drop from ~15% to ~2%. The rule "not a template. Personal" is in the document. It needs to be followed with discipline.

**Threat 3: Vanity metrics replacing activation metrics**
Total sign-up count is a vanity metric. An artist who signs up but never captures a fan is not an active user. The metrics table leads with "activated artists" (≥1 fan captured), not total sign-ups. This hierarchy must be maintained.

**Threat 4: Founding artist selection by follower count**
It is tempting to choose the 10 founding artists by social following. The specification explicitly says 1k–50k. An artist with 200k followers is not the right founding artist — they are probably already in discussions with a major tool and will not give the depth of feedback the product needs.

**Threat 5: Skipping the Monday review**
The weekly review is the forcing function that kills bad experiments and doubles down on what is working. If it becomes ad hoc, the growth system degrades into instinct-driven activity.
