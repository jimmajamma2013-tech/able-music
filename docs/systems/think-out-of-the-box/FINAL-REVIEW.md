Five questions that test whether this system will actually be used rather than read once and filed.

## Question 1: Can you describe the system in one sentence?

If you can't, it won't survive contact with a busy sprint.

**The answer that proves it works:**
"Once a month, spend two hours applying one of these ten techniques to ABLE, write the output in LATERAL-IDEAS.md, and check the graveyard before getting excited about any new idea."

That is one sentence. It contains the frequency (monthly), the time investment (two hours), the method (pick one technique), the output (an entry in the ideas log), and the maintenance habit (check the graveyard).

If you cannot say that fluently after reading the folder, the folder is too long. If you can, it will survive.

---

## Question 2: Does the system prevent the most common failure mode — or does it just document it?

The most common failure mode: the team gets excited about a new idea, adds it to the backlog, builds it, and discovers two months later that someone already discussed and rejected it six months ago. The institutional memory is gone and the team just repeated a loop.

**The graveyard** is the direct answer to this. It is not a retrospective. It is a pre-filter. Before any idea enters the backlog, it should be checked against the graveyard.

The system prevents the failure mode, not just documents it. The graveyard is active infrastructure, not a historical archive.

---

## Question 3: Will the ideas log grow meaningfully, or will it start strong and stall?

A living document that is seeded with 10 entries and never updated is worse than no document — it creates the illusion of a practice without the practice.

Three conditions that determine whether this log grows:

1. **The monthly session happens.** This is the only reliable growth mechanism. If sessions happen, entries accumulate. If sessions don't happen, the log dies.

2. **The threshold for adding an entry is low.** The template has six fields. That is the right level of friction — enough structure to make the entry useful, not so much that it feels like a form to fill in. An idea can be added in 10 minutes.

3. **Ideas that get built are tracked back to the log.** When an idea from this log makes it into the product, note it in the entry: `**Status**: Adopted — [feature name] launched [date]`. This creates a visible history of the system working, which reinforces the habit.

If all three conditions hold, the log will grow. The log growing is evidence the system is working.

---

## Question 4: Is this system compatible with the way ABLE currently operates?

ABLE is a single-developer + AI agents build. No formal sprints. No PM. No product board. Decisions happen quickly. The risk is that a system that requires formal scheduling will be deprioritised every time there's a sprint.

**The honest assessment:** This system requires James to protect two hours per month for non-building thinking time. That is the only ask. No meeting. No team. No deliverable. Just time.

Two hours per month is 24 hours per year. That is a reasonable price for a system that prevents the product from optimising into the wrong direction. The question is not whether it is worth it — it clearly is — but whether the calendar habit can be maintained under pressure.

One practical suggestion: schedule the session on the first Monday of every month, recurring. Make it immovable except for extraordinary circumstances (shipping day, investor meeting). Two hours on the first Monday of every month, calendar blocked.

That is the only process change this system requires.

---

## Question 5: What does failure look like, and will you know it when you see it?

Failure is not dramatic. It is gradual. It looks like:

- The LATERAL-IDEAS.md file hasn't been updated in 3 months.
- Every idea in the backlog is an iteration on something that already exists.
- A competitor launches a feature you "hadn't thought of" — but would have thought of, if you'd run a session in the last quarter.
- The graveyard hasn't been checked before a new feature was added to the backlog.
- The monthly sessions still happen on paper but have become a 20-minute skim rather than a real exercise.

**You'll know the system is working if:**
- At least one entry per month in LATERAL-IDEAS.md
- At least one idea per quarter makes it to "Exploring" status
- At least one idea per year makes it to "Adopted"
- The graveyard is referenced before at least one backlog conversation per quarter
- Someone says "have you checked the graveyard?" and it prevents a repeat discussion

The metric is not perfection — it is direction. If the system is producing more ideas than ABLE can act on, it's working. If it's producing zero ideas for months, it has failed and the question is why.
