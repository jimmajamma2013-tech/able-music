## What this growth system does better than what existed before

**1. It introduces the growth pyramid with explicit priority order.**
The most common early-stage mistake is running acquisition before retention is fixed. The pyramid addresses this directly. Retention sits at the base — it is the precondition for everything above it. This is not a feel-good principle; it is mathematics (churn above 5%/month means no acquisition channel can build a sustainable business).

**2. It identifies three distinct PLG loops.**
The "Made with ABLE" fan-to-artist loop was in the existing doc. The profile footer PLG loop and the fan confirmation email loop are new. All three are zero-marginal-cost mechanisms. They should be built and tracked before any paid acquisition is considered.

**3. It gives the growth metrics dashboard a kill-signal framework.**
The 8 metrics + 3 states (Green/Amber/Red) + weekly review rhythm gives James a decision system, not just a report card. The difference: a report card tells you what happened. A decision system tells you what to do about it.

**4. It addresses international sequencing with a specific Colombia rationale.**
UK → Colombia → US is a deliberate sequencing. Colombia as Latin American beachhead (not Brazil, not Mexico) is a considered choice based on the indie music culture, lower CAC, and James's existing interest in building for that market. This prevents the classic mistake of going "to the US" before the home market is proven.

**5. The "one channel principle" is the most actionable insight in the system.**
The prescription (find one channel below £5 CAC, then kill everything else) is counterintuitive and correct. It will save 6 months of misdirected effort.

## What this growth system doesn't do yet

**1. It hasn't produced a single paying artist.**
This is obvious but important to state. A growth strategy is a hypothesis until it's been tested. The quality of the strategy will be determined by what happens in the next 60 days when producer seeding begins and the first personal outreach goes out.

**2. The community sections are plans, not live systems.**
The Founding Artists programme, the Discord, the Abler tiers — all fully designed, none executing yet. These become real when the first 50 artists exist. Before that, they are well-designed infrastructure waiting for occupants.

**3. The international sequencing has no execution date.**
Colombia is Year 2, with specific preconditions (80%+ UK 12-month retention, one channel below £10 CAC UK, NPS above 50). These preconditions are currently unmet. The Colombia plan should be revisited in Q1 2027 against real numbers.

---

## The three most important things in this document

If James has 5 minutes before a meeting and needs to remember what matters:

**1. Retention before acquisition.**
If monthly churn is above 5%, no acquisition channel can build the business. Fix activation rate and 30-day retention first. Spend nothing on acquisition until churn is below 4%.

**2. Producer seeding is the Year 1 lever.**
Not SEO. Not paid ads. Not Product Hunt. Producers who work with multiple artists are the highest-leverage, lowest-cost acquisition source available. 20 producer DMs is the first action, not the first option.

**3. When one channel works below £5 CAC: stop testing, double down.**
The discipline of the single channel is the difference between a focused growth engine and a diffuse experiment that produces noise instead of signal.
