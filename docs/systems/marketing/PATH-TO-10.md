# ABLE Marketing System — Path to 10
**What makes this marketing system a 10/10 across every dimension**

---

## How to read this file

A 10 in marketing is not perfection — it is systematic clarity. Every angle has a clear definition of excellent, an honest assessment of where the gap is, and a specific path to close it.

---

## 1. Marketing Philosophy Alignment

**What a 10 looks like:** Every piece of content produced by ABLE — post, email, DM, waitlist confirmation, Product Hunt listing, Reddit comment — reads as if the same thoughtful artist wrote it. There is no tonal inconsistency between the product, the marketing, and the personal brand. A reader should not be able to distinguish "ABLE the product" from "James the artist-builder."

**Current state:** The philosophy is correctly defined. The risk is execution. A philosophy-aligned marketing system requires habits, not just principles.

**Path to 10:**
- Before writing any marketing copy, re-read the copy rules in CLAUDE.md or `docs/v6/core/COPY_AND_DESIGN_PHILOSOPHY.md`. This is not optional. The philosophy decays without reinforcement.
- Create a "before we post" check: read the copy out loud as if you are the artist it's targeting. Does it sound genuine? Does it make you want to act? Does it have even one word that a real artist wouldn't use? Fix that word.
- Annual review: pick 10 posts from the year and score them for philosophy alignment. If any feel like startup marketing, trace back to when the tone shifted and correct it.
- **The final test:** share the post with one artist who does not know it's from ABLE and ask "Does this sound like it was written by a real person who makes music?" If yes, it's right.

## 2. James's Artist Page as a Marketing Asset

**What a 10 looks like:** James's own ABLE page is the best artist page on the platform. Not "pretty good for a demo" — genuinely the best. Campaign state active. Real music embedded. Real fan sign-ups happening. When an artist clicks through from his Instagram bio and sees that page, the immediate response is "I want this."

**Current state:** The page exists at `able-v7.html`. It needs to be James's actual live page, not a demo with placeholder content.

**Path to 10:**
- Set a release date for real music — or use the gig mode for an actual show. The campaign state only demonstrates its value when it's live and real.
- Get real fan sign-ups. Even 20 people on the list. The fan list view in admin.html is part of the marketing story.
- When showing the page to artists: show it on mobile, not desktop. The artist target is always on their phone.
- Test it at every realistic scenario: on a slow 4G connection, with a first-time visitor who doesn't know what they're looking at, on an Android device.
- **The standard:** if an artist says "can I get one of these?" immediately after seeing it, the page is at 10. If they have any questions about what it is, it needs work.

## 3. Channel Discipline — Not Spreading Thin

**What a 10 looks like:** ABLE dominates 2–3 channels completely rather than being mediocre on 5. The target artist sees consistent, high-quality content on the channels they actually use. No ghost channels, no "we should post more on LinkedIn" distractions.

**Current state:** Spec correctly identifies Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, and email as the priority channels. The risk is spreading effort before establishing a beachhead channel.

**Path to 10:**
- For V1 (pre-100 artists): Instagram is channel 1. Post consistently, learn what resonates, build the format. TikTok is channel 2. Email is channel 3. Twitter/X is channel 4 — low effort, high potential yield with music industry contacts.
- No Pinterest, no LinkedIn, no YouTube (until a proper tutorial is ready), no Clubhouse revival or equivalent.
- Each channel has a 60-day review: is it generating waitlist sign-ups or artist sign-ups? If not, deprioritise it ruthlessly. No sunk cost reasoning.
- **The constraint that creates quality:** 4 posts per week on Instagram. If 4 quality posts per week is not achievable, do 2. Never post filler.

## 4. Waitlist Quality and Conversion

**What a 10 looks like:** 500 people on the waitlist before public launch. Not 500 generic sign-ups — 500 independent artists or people who work with artists. The launch email converts 20%+ of the waitlist to active users within the first week.

**Current state:** Waitlist form on landing.html. No waitlist contacts yet.

**Path to 10:**
- Every waitlist sign-up should come with a source UTM: instagram_bio, tiktok_bio, reddit_post, personal_outreach. This tells you what's working during the pre-launch period.
- Qualify the waitlist: add one optional field after email — "Are you an artist, a manager/label, or something else?" This does not reduce conversion meaningfully but gives a sense of the waitlist's composition.
- Nurture once, not constantly: send one email at 200 subscribers ("200 people are waiting — here's what I've built so far") and one at 500 ("We're almost ready. You'll hear first."). That's it. Do not run a drip campaign.
- The launch email: write it before you hit 500. Edit it three times. Read it as if you received it having signed up 6 weeks ago. Does it feel worth the wait?
- **Target signal:** launch week conversion (waitlist to active user) above 20%. Below 15% suggests the waitlist expectation wasn't set correctly.

## 5. Producer Seeding as Marketing Infrastructure

**What a 10 looks like:** 20 UK producers have free Artist Pro accounts. They are actively using ABLE for their own profiles. 5 of them have directly introduced ABLE to their artist clients. At least 3 of those artists have live ABLE pages. The producer channel is ABLE's most cost-efficient acquisition mechanism.

**Current state:** Strategy defined. No producer relationships formalised yet.

**Path to 10:**
- Create a shortlist of 20 UK producers: real names, contact info, mutual connections. Not a spreadsheet pulled from Spotify — people James knows or is one introduction away from.
- Personal outreach: not a template. Each message acknowledges something specific about the producer's work. "I heard your work on [track]. I built something for this." Two sentences maximum before the ask.
- The ask: "I'd like to give you free Artist Pro while I'm building this. Would you be up for a call to see it first?" — not "beta test," not "would you be interested," not a pitch deck.
- Success metric for the seeding phase: 3 months post-seeding, do 15 of the 20 still have active pages? Are any of them mentioning ABLE to their artists without being asked?
- **The compound effect:** each producer introduction is worth approximately 3–10 new artist sign-ups if the product delivers. At 5 active producers referring artists: 15–50 new artists in 90 days at zero paid acquisition cost.

## 6. Community Building (Discord)

**What a 10 looks like:** The ABLE Discord has 200+ members. The #page-feedback channel has 3–5 posts per week with genuine, helpful feedback from other artists. Artists are discovering each other through the community. ABLE gets mentioned in conversations as "the thing" the community is built around, not as the thing that runs the community.

**Current state:** Discord launch planned at 50 artists. Not yet live.

**Path to 10:**
- Do not launch Discord before 50 active artist pages. An empty community is worse than no community.
- Seed the community: invite the 20 producers first. Their presence gives the Discord credibility before it has scale.
- Set the culture in the first 30 days: #page-feedback should have James's own page in it on day 1 asking for feedback. Model the behaviour you want.
- Hire a community manager at Month 12 if Discord grows beyond 500 members. Communities die when founders disappear and there's no one to moderate.
- **The test:** after 90 days, are there conversations happening without ABLE initiating them? If yes, the community has a life of its own. If no, reconsider the channel.

## 7. SEO as a Long-Term Compound

**What a 10 looks like:** By end of Year 2, ABLE ranks on page 1 for "link in bio for musicians" and "Linktree for artists." Organic search is generating 30%+ of new sign-ups. Each artist profile is indexed and generating a small but compounding flow of search traffic.

**Current state:** Technical SEO foundation described in spec. Not yet implemented.

**Path to 10:**
- OG tags: implement on all pages at launch. Non-negotiable.
- Google Search Console: set up and submit sitemap on launch day. Monitor impressions vs. clicks for target keywords from week 1.
- Artist profile SEO: each ablemusic.co/[name] page must have correct meta tags. This is passive but compounds. At 500 artist profiles, 500 indexed pages with unique content.
- Blog: commit to 10 posts by end of Year 1. Not 10 ideas — 10 published posts. Write them as an artist, not as a tech company. The post about "what to do on release day" should be something an artist would forward to another artist.
- **Patient game:** SEO takes 6–12 months to show meaningful results. Plant the seeds now. Do not optimise for traffic before you have conversion data — get the conversion funnel right first, then drive traffic.

## 8. Content Consistency and Format Discipline

**What a 10 looks like:** ABLE's content is recognisably ABLE. The visual style (dark, editorial, music-first), the copy register, and the subject matter are consistent across posts, channels, and months. A new follower can scroll back 6 months and the brand reads the same.

**Current state:** Philosophy defined. No content calendar yet.

**Path to 10:**
- Build a content template: a set of 5–8 post formats that work. Not rigid templates — formats. "Artist spotlight reel: 45-second format with their music + a quote from them." Once a format works, repeat it. Originality is for the content, not the container.
- Visual consistency: use the same colour palette (ABLE dark system), same fonts (Barlow Condensed for headings), same aspect ratios. Not because it looks corporate — because consistency builds recognition.
- Content bank: maintain a rolling 2-week content bank. Never posting in real time under pressure. Batch content creation (one morning per week) and schedule.
- Monthly content review: pick 5 posts from the month, score them. What performed? What resonated? What felt off-brand? Adjust, don't abandon.
- **The standard:** an artist following ABLE for 6 months should feel like they know the person behind it. Not a brand — a perspective.

## 9. Artist Success Stories as Evidence

**What a 10 looks like:** ABLE's marketing features real, named artists with real results. "Sarah built her email list to 200 fans during her EP release. Every one of those emails is hers." These stories are more persuasive than any feature announcement.

**Current state:** No artists yet. This pillar activates at 10+ active artists.

**Path to 10:**
- From sign-up 1: ask every early artist for permission to feature their story. Not at sign-up — at the moment they have a result (first 50 fans, first release launch with ABLE, first gig mode activation). That's the story.
- Build a "success story" template: artist name, music genre, specific moment, specific result, quote. Keep it short. No case study format.
- Feature on landing.html social proof section: 3 quotes from real artists. Not "changed my relationship with my fans" vague praise — specific, concrete, honest. "I got 47 email sign-ups during my release week. My Linktree gave me 0."
- **The test:** when a new artist lands on landing.html and reads the success stories, do they recognise themselves in the featured artists? If yes, the stories are working.

## 10. Launch Moment Quality

**What a 10 looks like:** The launch week is deliberate, not frantic. The waitlist email converts at 20%+. The Reddit post gets genuine engagement (not upvote manipulation). The Product Hunt listing looks real. At least one music publication writes something. The first week generates 100+ sign-ups.

**Current state:** Launch sequence is documented. Not yet executed.

**Path to 10:**
- Prepare every piece of launch collateral at least 2 weeks before launch: waitlist email (written, reviewed, scheduled), Reddit post (written, read against subreddit tone, ready to post), Product Hunt assets (screenshots, tagline, description, hunter confirmed).
- The Reddit post is the highest-risk piece of launch collateral. Artists on r/WeAreTheMusicMakers are highly attuned to spam and self-promotion dressed as sharing. The post must be: "I'm an artist, I had this specific problem, I built something, here it is." No "we," no "ABLE's mission," no feature list. Personal. Specific. Brief.
- Product Hunt timing: launch on a Tuesday or Wednesday (highest traffic days). Have 10 people ready to upvote and comment authentically — not all at once, spread over the morning.
- Press: the realistic expectation is that DIY Magazine or The Quietus won't write about ABLE at launch. What might happen: a brief mention in a music tech newsletter, a social post from a music journalist who finds it interesting. That's a success. Don't optimise for press — optimise for genuine user sign-ups.
- **Post-launch review at 7 days:** how many sign-ups from each channel? What was the waitlist conversion rate? Was there any moment that overperformed? Double down on that. Underperforming channels: park them, don't fix them.
