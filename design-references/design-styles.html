01

Swiss / International Typographic

Medium relevance

Extreme restraint. Asymmetric grids aligned to an invisible underlying structure. Black or single-accent type on white. Every element has a reason to exist and a precise position. Photography used as documentary fact, never decorative.

Direct lineage from Bauhaus (1919–1933). Josef Müller-Brockmann, Armin Hofmann, Emil Ruder. The intellectual DNA of Stripe, Linear, and Vercel.

02

Glassmorphism

★ High — ABLE uses this

Frosted glass panels floating over blurred gradient backgrounds. Semi-transparent surfaces with visible backdrop-blur. Subtle white border on the glass edge catches light. Background must always be vivid — the blur needs material to work with.

Apple macOS Big Sur (2020) brought it mainstream. ABLE's background-image theme is a direct application of this technique. Now a settled standard, not a trend.

Consumer apps, music/entertainment, lifestyle, creative tools

03

Dark Luxury / Dark Mode Premium

★ High — ABLE's aesthetic

Deep near-black backgrounds (#0a0a0a, not pure #000). Gold, platinum, or monochrome accents. Ultra-thin typography with generous tracking. Photography: low-key lighting, cinematic. Negative space used lavishly. Micro-animations slow and deliberate.

Luxury watches, high-end fashion (Net-A-Porter, Mr Porter), premium automotive. Accelerated by OS-level dark mode adoption (2018–2019). The definitive premium UI language.

04

Bento Grid Layout

★ High — feature sections

Named after the Japanese bento box. A mosaic of rectangular cards, each containing one piece of content. Cells are different sizes — some span multiple columns — but fit together like a puzzle. Spatial hierarchy through cell size, not visual weight.

Apple WWDC23 feature pages launched this into mainstream design consciousness. Now standard for SaaS feature highlights and "about" pages.

MosaicGridCardsApple-styleFeature-highlights

CSS technique

display: grid;
grid-template-columns: repeat(12, 1fr);
.hero { grid-column: span 8; grid-row: span 2; }

Best for

SaaS feature highlights, portfolio overviews, product landing pages, company "about" sections

Trend status

Peak standard (2023–∞)

05

Minimalism / White Space

Medium relevance

Almost nothing on the page. A single word or image surrounded by vast white space. If something can be removed without losing meaning, it is removed. Breathing room is the design itself. Dieter Rams, John Pawson, Japanese Ma.

One typeface (or one serif + one sans). Extreme margins. Nothing is small — everything that stays is large enough to own its space.

WhitespaceRestrainedSingularBreathing-roomConfident

Best for

Independent designers, architects, luxury single-SKU products, premium food brands, writing/essays

06

Aurora / Gradient Mesh

★ High — background themes

Smooth large-scale colour gradients that blend across the entire screen — like the Northern Lights. Colours shift from one hue to another across enormous distances. Background is the hero; UI elements float on top as clean flat components.

Stripe's animated gradient homepage is the definitive web implementation. Instagram's 2016 rebrand popularised gradient-as-primary-brand-element.

GradientMeshAuroraColour-blendAtmospheric

CSS technique

radial-gradient(ellipse at 80% 20%, #db2777 0%, transparent 50%);

Best for

SaaS, tech, social platforms, consumer apps, anything needing warmth and energy

07

Spatial / AI-Native UI

★ High — future direction

Sparse, near-empty interfaces designed for screens that don't yet exist. Thin UI chrome — only the minimum container needed. Information appears on demand. Deep depth — elements at different perceived distances, as if viewed through spatial computing glasses.

Apple Vision Pro (2024) establishes the template. Perplexity, Cursor, Arc, Linear all adopt this language. The definitive emerging direction for premium AI-native software.

SpatialSparseGlassDepthAI-native

CSS technique

backdrop-filter: blur(40px);
border: 0.5px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.12);
border-radius: 24px;

Best for

AI tools, spatial computing apps, deep-tech, creative tools, premium developer tools

Trend status

Emerging dominant (2024–∞)

08

Cyberpunk / Neon Noir

Medium — electronic music

Dark near-black backgrounds. Neon accent colours — electric cyan, hot magenta, acid green. Glitch effects on type and images (RGB channel split). Scan lines. HUD-style overlays. Monospaced type. Elements feel like a futuristic OS in a rain-soaked city.

Blade Runner, Neuromancer, Ghost in the Shell, The Matrix. Game UI (Deus Ex, Cyberpunk 2077). Beatport and electronic music UI use this vocabulary. Sustained niche with major spikes around gaming releases.

Gaming, crypto/DeFi, electronic/industrial music, sci-fi entertainment

09

Y2K / Retro-Futurism

Medium — music artists

Late 1990s and early 2000s aesthetics imagining the future. Shiny chrome type with gradients. Metallics — silver, iridescent, holographic. Oval shapes, 3D bubble objects. A palette that is simultaneously naïve and futuristic: electric blue, lilac, silver, hot pink.

Revived by GenZ nostalgia (~2020–present). Charli XCX, Caroline Polachek, 100 Gecs aesthetic references. Active in music, fashion, beauty targeting Gen Z audiences.

Pop/hyperpop music artists, Gen Z beauty brands, nightlife, event identity

10

Claymorphism

Low — not ABLE's register

Rounded, puffy 3D-looking shapes that appear to be made of coloured clay or soft plastic. Very high border-radius, bright saturated colours, thick coloured inner glow suggests inflated volume. Friendly, toy-like. Often combined with bold drop shadows.

Emerged 2021–2022, driven by Apple's redesigned iOS emoji. Duolingo owl is the most recognisable example. Becoming associated with a specific moment — will age.

PuffyRounded3D-toyClayPlayful

Key CSS

box-shadow: 0 20px 40px rgba(color, 0.4), inset 0 -4px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.15), inset 0 4px 8px rgba(255,255,255,0.4);

Best for

Children's apps, gaming, food brands, health/wellness consumer apps

Trend status

Peak 2022–2024, will date

11

Editorial / Long-Form Web

★ High — ABLE's content mode

Treats the browser as a magazine page. Full-bleed imagery alternates with dense typographic sections. Pull quotes in oversized display type break the text flow. Drop caps. Art-directed photography with specific bleeds and crops.

NYT Snowfall (2012) is the founding document. The Pudding, Bloomberg Visual Stories. ABLE's "About" sections and long-form artist storytelling naturally inhabit this aesthetic.

12

Maximalism / Anti-Minimalism

Medium — artist expression

More of everything. Multiple typefaces in one composition. Overlapping elements. Loud colour combinations. Dense, information-rich layouts. Visual rhythm through organised chaos — there is a system, but it is complex. David Carson's Ray Gun magazine.

GenZ aesthetic codes favour maximalism. Fashion brands and music industry are primary adopters. Dazed Digital, i-D Magazine, Dover Street Market. Growing as counter to the Apple/Linear minimalism.

DenseLayeredExpressiveAnti-minimalChaotic

Best for

Music artists (hip-hop, pop, experimental), fashion brands, cultural events, festival identity, streetwear

13

Typographic / Type-First Design

Medium — hero moments

Typography is the entire design. Giant type that fills the full viewport. Words as visual objects, not just carriers of meaning. Letter-spacing pushed to extremes. Headlines might be 20vw. Every pixel decision is a typographic decision.

Made practical by variable fonts (OpenType 2016), CSS viewport units, and scroll-driven animation APIs. Active Theory, Fantasy, Pentagram, Wieden+Kennedy. High-craft respected niche — requires genuine typographic knowledge.

KineticVariable-fontsLetterformType-as-imageTypographic

Key CSS

font-size: clamp(4rem, 15vw, 20rem);
font-variation-settings: "wght" var(--w);
animation-timeline: scroll();

Best for

Design agencies, cultural institutions, music/editorial brands, fashion, independent artists

Trend status

Growing (variable fonts era)

14

Web Brutalism

Niche — experimental artists

Intentional "ugliness" as aesthetic. Raw HTML-era aesthetics deliberately deployed: visible structure, exposed function, no decorative smoothing. Times New Roman. Black borders. Primary colours without curation. Deliberately unfinished.

Counter-movement to homogenised startup/Bootstrap aesthetic (~2014–2016). Balenciaga.com uses luxury brutalism — raw black, extreme spacing. Increasingly adopted by experimental music artists.

Cultural/artistic institutions, punk/experimental music labels, zines, creative agencies wanting anti-corporate identity

15

Neumorphism (Soft UI)

Niche — music player widgets

Elements appear extruded from or pressed into the background. Everything is the same colour. Two shadows per element: light (top-left, white) and dark (bottom-right), creating a bas-relief, tactile, physical feel. Very soft, pillowy, quiet.

Peaked 2019–2021. Significant accessibility criticism (contrast ratios fail WCAG). Niche use in prototype/concept work. Still instructive as a shadow technique — the dual-shadow principle is worth knowing.

Soft-UIShadowsMonochromaticTactileBas-relief

Key CSS

box-shadow: 6px 6px 12px #b8bec7, -6px -6px 12px #ffffff;
/* active */ box-shadow: inset 4px 4px 8px #b8bec7, inset -4px -4px 8px #fff;

Best for

Smart home dashboards, wearable companion apps, music player widgets, productivity widgets

16

Flat Design 2.0

Medium — dashboard/stats

Pure two-dimensional. No gradients, no shadows, no textures, no bevels. Shapes are clean geometric fills. Colour is bold and saturated. Treats the screen as a flat surface, not a simulation of three-dimensional space.

Flat 2.0 includes subtle shadows for depth cues. Microsoft Metro / Windows Phone (2010) pioneered it. Apple iOS 7 (2013) mainstream adoption. Permanent baseline for dashboards and data-dense UI.

17

Material Design

Low — Android ecosystem

Flat design with a physics layer. Elements cast shadows that communicate their elevation on a z-axis. Cards rise above surfaces. Ripple animations radiate from tap points. Material You (M3) derives color from the user's wallpaper — tonal palettes generated algorithmically.

Google's design language launched 2014. Based on "paper and ink" metaphor. Dominant across Android ecosystem. Material 3 / Material You is the current evolution.

Best for

Android apps, cross-platform Google products, enterprise dashboards, productivity tools

18

Skeuomorphism

Niche — audio plugin UIs

UI elements that look exactly like their real-world physical counterparts. Every surface has texture — felt, leather, wood grain, metal brushing, paper. Buttons have bevels and gradients to suggest three-dimensional objects. The notes app looks like a yellow legal pad.

Apple under Steve Jobs (2007–2013). Mostly historical in mainstream UI. Returns selectively: luxury products, audio hardware UIs (UAD, Arturia V Collection — simulate hardware exactly), games, VR/AR.

RealisticTexturedPhysical-metaphorTactileImmersive

Best for

Games and game UIs, luxury brand experiences, themed apps, VR/AR interfaces, premium audio equipment software

Trend status

Historical · Niche revival

19

Morphic / Organic / Bio Design

Niche — wellness context

Shapes that look like they grew rather than were drawn. Fluid, asymmetric blobs. Natural textures — marble, stone, plant. Earthy colour palettes. Soft, irregular, never perfectly geometric. Feels like a natural object placed on screen.

Biophilia — humans are neurologically predisposed to find natural shapes calming. Wellness industry adopted it first. Headspace, Calm, Frank Body, Aesop. Peaked 2020–2023 for wellness brands. Enduring in wellness; spreading to sustainability and food.

Best for

Wellness and mental health apps, organic food brands, cosmetics, sustainability, yoga/meditation

20

Memphis / Post-Modern Graphic

Niche — festival branding

Geometric shapes in primary and secondary colours, scattered without apparent functional purpose. Squiggles, dots, stripes, checkers — decorative and playful, like a 1980s kitchen. Bold outlines on shapes. Pattern and colour as the primary visual language.

Memphis Group, Italy (1981–1988) — Ettore Sottsass. Revived in 2014–2018 especially on Instagram. Mailchimp and Slack illustration style draws heavily from it. The Barbie movie (2023) caused a pastel Memphis/80s revival.

GeometricPlayful80sPatternMemphis-Group

Best for

Lifestyle brands, food/beverage, music festival identity, retail targeting millennials, social media templates

Trend status

Niche · Periodic revival
